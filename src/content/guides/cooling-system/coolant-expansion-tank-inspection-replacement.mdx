## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

Expansion-tank faults are often mistaken for unrelated coolant loss or gauge behavior issues. Diagnose carefully before replacing parts.

> Warning: Only inspect/open cooling components when the system is fully cool to avoid burns from pressurized hot coolant. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

> High-risk quote: If heater/cooling issues are linked to low coolant, poor system filling/bleeding, or leaks, engine temperature control can become unreliable. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/car-heater-not-working

> Manual-reference quote: NB service documentation is year-grouped (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact hose routing, level checks, and refill/bleed details must be confirmed by year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Flashlight and mirror
- Basic hand tools for hose clamps and tank fasteners
- Drain pan and absorbent rags
- Cooling-system pressure tester (recommended)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct expansion/recovery tank for NB variant
- New hose clamps if originals are weak/corroded
- Correct coolant for top-up/refill

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Same diagnostic approach: inspect tank body/seams/neck/hoses and verify level behavior across heat cycles.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same fundamentals, but bracket/hose orientation can vary by year and market.
- Verify reservoir line routing and bleed sequence in your FSM.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptom pattern first

- Repeated low coolant in reservoir?
- Coolant smell near engine bay after hot soak?
- Wet residue/staining around tank seam, hose neck, or cap area?
- Temperature gauge behavior inconsistent with expected warm-up?

### 2) Visual inspection of tank and hoses

- Inspect plastic for yellowing, stress lines, seam weep marks, and small cracks.
- Check hose necks for brittleness or hairline splits.
- Inspect hose clamps and return hose condition.

### 3) Pressure-test confirmation (preferred)

- Pressure-test cooling system cold at appropriate cap/system rating.
- Observe for tank seam or neck seepage under pressure.

> High-risk quote: Cooling-system diagnosis should verify leak source and pressure behavior rather than replacing parts by guesswork. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/car-heater-not-working

### 4) Replace tank if cracked, warped, or leaking

- Capture any spilled coolant.
- Transfer hoses carefully and avoid over-tightening old brittle fittings.
- Install new tank, ensuring proper seating and hose routing.

### 5) Refill and bleed correctly

- Top up/refill with correct coolant.
- Follow FSM bleed process for your exact NB variant.
- Recheck level after full heat cycle and cooldown.

> Community quote: Miata owners discussing coolant-tank concerns repeatedly report that apparent “random coolant loss” often requires methodical cooling-system verification after tank work, not single-step assumptions. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=coolant+tank&restrict_sr=on

> Community confirmation quote: MX-5Nutz troubleshooting around cooling behavior and gauge anomalies highlights that electrical/gauge observations can mislead unless cooling-system integrity is confirmed first. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/coolant-expansion-tank.390650/

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Expansion tank mounting/clamp and refill details vary by year/market setup.
- Use year/VIN FSM for any torque values and final coolant fill/bleed specifications.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No fresh coolant residue around reservoir seams, neck, or hose joints.
- Coolant level remains stable through multiple heat cycles.
- Temperature behavior is stable in traffic and cruise.
- Cabin heater performance remains normal (no sudden loss from trapped air).

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources strongly support diagnose-first leak isolation, but universal NB-specific numeric thresholds for reservoir-only diagnosis are limited in openly accessible references.
- Article therefore emphasizes process reliability (pressure test, visual evidence, repeat heat-cycle checks) and defers exact year/VIN details to FSM.
