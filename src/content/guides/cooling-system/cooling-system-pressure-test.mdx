## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

A pressure test helps find leaks that may not appear during a quick visual check. Always start with a cold engine.

> Warning: Never open the cooling system when hot; verify pressure is gone first and release cap pressure slowly. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/radiator-pressure-test

> High-risk quote: Cooling systems are designed to operate under pressure, and the pressure raises coolant boiling point; wrong pressure behavior can cause overheating and damage. https://natrad.com.au/info-advice/how-do-you-pressure-test-a-cooling-system/

> Manual-reference quote: NB service procedures are split across year-group manuals (1999-2001 and 2005 sets), so exact test limits and follow-up checks must be confirmed by year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Cooling-system pressure tester with correct adapter for NB radiator neck or expansion-tank interface
- Flashlight and inspection mirror
- Gloves, eye protection, rags
- Drain pan (if leak opens significantly during testing)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Distilled water/correct coolant mix for top-up after diagnosis
- Replacement clamps/hoses/seals only after leak is confirmed

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Test logic is identical: cold system, pressurize to spec cap/system value, observe pressure drop and leak path.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same test method, but fan/auxiliary layout and surrounding components can vary by year and market.
- VVT presence does not change pressure-test fundamentals.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Baseline visual inspection (cold engine)

- Check radiator tanks/seams, upper/lower hoses, heater hose areas, water-pump weep region, and expansion bottle.
- Look for dried coolant staining before applying pressure.

### 2) Install tester correctly

- Remove cap only on fully cold engine.
- Fit the correct adapter and verify a sealed connection.

> High-risk quote: The tester must seal at radiator or reservoir neck to simulate operating expansion pressure across hoses, heater core, block, and radiator. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/radiator-pressure-test

### 3) Pressurize gradually to the correct range

- Pump slowly and do **not** exceed cap/system rating from FSM/cap marking.
- Hold pressure and watch gauge behavior for several minutes.

> High-risk quote: Typical cooling-system operating/test ranges are often around 12-16 psi (vehicle-specific), and over-pressurizing can create false failures. https://natrad.com.au/info-advice/how-do-you-pressure-test-a-cooling-system/

### 4) Diagnose by leak pattern

- **Stable pressure + no visible leak:** recheck cap function and inspect for intermittent leaks during heat cycles.
- **Slow pressure drop + wet traces:** trace to hose joint, radiator seam/neck, heater circuit, or pump area.
- **Rapid pressure drop:** likely obvious external leak or major sealing issue.

### 5) Escalation checks when no external leak is obvious

If pressure behavior is abnormal but no clear external source appears, escalate to combustion-gas/head-gasket-focused diagnostics before replacing parts blindly.

> Community quote: MX-5 owners reporting frothy coolant and continuing loss without obvious external leaks often get advised toward deeper head-gasket/combustion-gas diagnosis. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/frothy-coolant-and-questionable-compression-test.390536/

> Community confirmation quote: Community troubleshooting also highlights that gauge behavior/fan behavior can mislead, and a methodical cooling diagnosis is needed instead of guessing from dash temperature alone. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/coolant-problem-to-pressure.102900/

### 6) Reassemble and verify after repairs

- Depressurize tester before removal.
- Refit cap and top up correctly.
- Run heat cycle and recheck for leaks and stable level.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Pressure-test target value: follow radiator cap/FSM year-VIN specification.
- No single universal numeric value is published here for all NB market variants.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Coolant level remains stable across several heat cycles.
- No fresh coolant odor/residue at leak-prone points.
- Cabin heater performance remains consistent (no sudden air pockets).
- Temperature behavior remains normal in traffic and steady cruise.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources are aligned on method but vary on exact hold-time expectations and acceptable pressure-drop threshold.
- Article therefore emphasizes leak localization workflow over one fixed universal “pass/fail” number.
- Final threshold interpretation should use year/VIN FSM plus symptom context.
