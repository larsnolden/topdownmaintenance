## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

A weak radiator cap can cause coolant loss and temperature instability even when radiator, hoses, and pump are otherwise fine.

- Work only on a fully cold engine.
- Open cap in two stages (detent first, then full removal).
- Never exceed cap-rated pressure during testing.

## Required Tools

- Cooling-system pressure tester with cap-test adapter
- Flashlight
- Clean rag, gloves, eye protection

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Replacement radiator cap with **matching pressure rating** (kPa/bar/psi)
- Coolant for top-up if needed

## Where the cap is on NB and how it looks

On NB cars, the radiator cap is on top of the radiator at the front of engine bay.

Visual cues:
- round metal cap,
- warning text on top,
- two side ears for push-and-turn lock,
- spring-loaded center underside.

## How to open safely (cold only)

1. Place folded rag over cap.
2. Push down and rotate counter-clockwise to first stop.
3. Wait for any residual venting.
4. Push down again and remove fully.

## Read cap rating before testing/buying

Common markings:
- **88 kPa (~0.9 bar / 13 psi)**
- **108 kPa (~1.1 bar / 16 psi)**

Always match the cap rating on your car/FSM unless your cooling setup is intentionally re-engineered.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Baseline symptom check

Typical cap-related symptoms:
- coolant pushed to overflow bottle too easily,
- repeated low level after cooldown,
- residue/wetness around neck/cap area,
- temperature fluctuation at idle/traffic.

### 2) Inspect cap and neck physically

Check cap:
- rubber seals not cracked/hardened/swollen,
- spring not rusty or weak,
- ears not bent.

Check radiator neck:
- sealing lip smooth and clean,
- no chips/pits/cracks,
- tabs intact so cap locks fully.

### 3) Pressure-test the cap

- Mount cap on tester adapter.
- Pump gradually toward rated pressure.
- Observe hold/release behavior.

Practical pass/fail guidance:
- Holds near rating without rapid drop = generally usable.
- Releases too early, won’t hold, or leaks down rapidly = replace.

### 4) Install replacement cap correctly

- Clean neck lip and tabs.
- Seat cap squarely.
- Push and rotate to full lock stop (not half-locked).

### 5) Post-install verification

- Warm engine to operating temperature.
- Confirm no fresh residue around neck/cap.
- Recheck coolant level next cold cycle.

If coolant loss/overheat behavior remains, continue with full cooling pressure test and leak diagnosis.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No fresh coolant residue at cap/neck
- Stable coolant level over 2-3 heat cycles
- Stable temperature in traffic and cruise
