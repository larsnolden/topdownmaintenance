## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

Radiator cap faults can mimic bigger cooling problems. Always test and inspect on a cold engine.

> Warning: Start with a cold engine and release/remove the cap slowly only after confirming no residual system pressure. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/radiator-pressure-test

> High-risk quote: Cooling-system pressure is controlled by cap/system design, and wrong pressure behavior can lead to overheating and component damage. https://natrad.com.au/info-advice/how-do-you-pressure-test-a-cooling-system/

> Manual-reference quote: NB service info is year-grouped (1999-2001 and 2005 manuals), so cap pressure spec and replacement details should be confirmed by exact year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Correct cap tester or cooling-system pressure tester with cap-test adapter
- Flashlight
- Gloves, eye protection, clean rag

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Replacement radiator cap with the **same pressure rating printed on your current cap** (match bar/kPa/psi marking exactly unless FSM says otherwise)
- Coolant for top-up if needed

### How to identify the right cap before buying

Read the markings stamped/printed on the cap top. Typical cap labels show pressure in one or more units:
- kPa (for example 88 or 108)
- bar (for example 0.9 or 1.1)
- psi (for example 13 or 16)

For a stock road car, match the original rating rather than “upgrading” pressure.

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Same cap-test logic as NB2: verify seal condition and pressure-hold/release behavior.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same method; verify cap pressure rating by year/market documentation.
- Do not fit “higher pressure for performance” caps unless entire cooling system condition and spec implications are understood.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 0) Find and identify the radiator cap correctly

On NB cars, the radiator cap is the **metal spring-pressure cap on top of the radiator**, at the very front of the engine bay.

What it looks like:
- round metal cap with warning text,
- two locking ears,
- spring-loaded center section.

How it opens safely (cold engine only):
1. Place a thick rag over the cap.
2. Push down and rotate counter-clockwise to the **first stop** (pressure-release detent).
3. Wait for any residual pressure to vent.
4. Push down again and continue turning to remove fully.

### 1) Initial symptom check

Look for:
- Coolant loss with no obvious large leak
- Overflow behavior anomalies
- Temperature fluctuations after warm-up
- Coolant residue around cap/neck

### 2) Visual inspection of cap and neck

- Inspect cap rubber seals for cracks/hardening.
- Check spring action and corrosion.
- Inspect radiator neck sealing surfaces.

> High-risk quote: A failed cap can prevent proper pressure control and cause overheating or coolant loss behavior. https://askmyauto.com/how-to-test-a-radiator-cap/

### 3) Test cap opening/holding pressure

- Use cap tester and pump to rated pressure.
- Confirm cap holds near its rating and releases correctly.
- Compare to cap marking and FSM expectation.

> High-risk quote: Cap testing is performed by pumping to the cap’s rated pressure and checking whether pressure holds; failure to hold indicates replacement. https://askmyauto.com/how-to-test-a-radiator-cap/

### 4) Replace cap if out-of-spec or physically degraded

Replace the cap if any of these are true:
- pressure test fails to hold near rated value,
- cap opens too early or does not release cleanly,
- rubber seals are cracked/hardened/swollen,
- spring feels weak or cap does not lock positively.

Installation checks:
- filler neck sealing surfaces clean and undamaged,
- cap ears fully under neck tabs,
- cap rotated to full lock stop (not half-locked).

### 5) Recheck cooling behavior

- Warm engine to operating temp.
- Verify stable temperature and normal overflow/recovery behavior.
- If issue persists, continue with full cooling-system pressure test.

> Community quote: MX-5 community troubleshooting repeatedly recommends full-system diagnosis when coolant behavior remains abnormal after initial fixes, rather than assuming one component is always the root cause. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/coolant-problem-to-pressure.102900/

> Community confirmation quote: Community owners also emphasize monitoring level/temperature trends over multiple drives to confirm whether a low-coolant concern is truly resolved. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/low-coolant.48908/

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Radiator cap is a hand-fit locking component (no torque value used).
- Pressure rating must match year/VIN FSM and cap specification.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No fresh coolant residue around cap/neck after heat cycles.
- Coolant level in radiator/expansion system remains stable.
- Temperature behavior is stable in idle and normal driving.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources agree on test principle and safety process.
- Cap pressure rating can vary by market/year/aftermarket radiator neck, so this guide requires matching the actual cap/FSM marking rather than publishing one universal NB pressure number.
