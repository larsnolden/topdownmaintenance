## How to use this appendix

This table is a **workshop planning sheet** for Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005). Use it to pre-buy fluids and avoid generic “correct fluid” guesswork.

- Treat values as practical baselines.
- Confirm final specs for your exact year/market/VIN from owner/FSM documentation.
- If your car has swapped drivetrain components, ignore stock capacities and use component-specific data.

## Baseline NB fluid/spec table

| System | Baseline spec to target | Typical quantity to have on hand | Practical purchase recommendation |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Engine coolant | Ethylene-glycol coolant safe for aluminum engines; avoid mixing chemistries | **6.0 L total system** (buy 6-7 L equivalent) | 6 L premix, or 3 L concentrate + 3-4 L distilled water |
| Engine oil + filter | Viscosity per climate/use and manual | ~4-5 L (varies by service state) | Buy 5 L jug + filter + crush washer |
| Manual transmission oil | Use gearbox-compatible MTF/GL fluid per year manual | ~2-3 L typical service planning stock | Buy 3 L so top-up and spillage are covered |
| Differential oil | Hypoid gear oil grade/spec per diff type | ~1-2 L typical service planning stock | Buy 2 L to ensure full fill with margin |
| Brake fluid | DOT spec from reservoir cap/manual (commonly DOT 3/4 family) | 1 L for full flush margin | Buy 1 L sealed bottle; discard leftovers after opening |
| Clutch fluid | Same reservoir-cap/manual DOT spec as system requires | Usually covered by brake-fluid purchase | Plan clutch bleed during brake service |
| Power steering | ATF M-III or Dexron II-equivalent language appears in NB references | 1 L is usually enough for check/top-up and minor bleed | Buy 1 L compatible ATF |

## Product examples (NB community/common-market)

These are examples, not sponsorships:

- **Coolant:** Comma Xstream G30, Comma Super Coldmaster, Mazda-equivalent matching chemistry
- **Power steering ATF:** Any reputable ATF explicitly stating Dexron II/III compatibility
- **Brake/clutch fluid:** DOT fluid matching cap/manual spec from major brands (ATE, Motul, Castrol, etc.)

## Consumables you should always stock

- Oil drain plug crush washers
- Hose clamps (assorted sizes)
- Brake cleaner
- Nitrile gloves
- Distilled water (at least 5 L)
- Shop towels/rags
- Funnel set (including spill-free coolant funnel)

## Logging format (recommended)

For each service, record:

1. Date + odometer
2. Fluid brand + exact product name
3. Quantity used
4. Observed condition (clean/dark/metallic/contaminated)
5. Next due date or mileage

This gives you traceability when diagnosing shift quality, cooling performance, steering noise, or brake feel changes.

## Notes on uncertainty

- NB published values can vary slightly by market and manual edition.
- “Dry fill” and “service refill” are different; most DIY jobs are service refills.
- If two sources conflict, use the cap label and factory documentation for your VIN.
