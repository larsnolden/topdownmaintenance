## Before You Start / Safety

- Work in a well-ventilated area.
- Wear eye protection and gloves.
- Keep sparks/flames away from the battery area.

> Warning: "When working with a battery wear protective eyewear and gloves... A battery is filled with explosive gasses that may ignite when extreme heat or a spark is present." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/everything-goes-dead-when-engine-is-cranked

## Required Tools

- Gloves and safety glasses
- Nylon/wire brush (battery terminal brush preferred)
- Baking soda + clean/distilled water
- Clean rags/paper towels
- Small socket/spanner for terminal clamps

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Battery terminal protectant (optional)
- Replacement terminal clamps/cables if damaged
- Replacement battery if testing confirms failure

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 and NB2 are both trunk-battery cars in most markets; condition of terminal cleanliness and cable integrity is often as important as battery age.
- Always confirm terminal orientation and cable routing before reconnecting.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "NB battery tender question ... which one of these is the positive terminal?" https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB+battery+terminal&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Initial battery health triage

Look for:
- slow crank,
- repeated low-voltage starts after parking,
- visible case bulging/leak evidence,
- heavy terminal corrosion.

If the battery repeatedly goes low, do not assume corrosion is the only issue; check charging and parasitic draw as needed.

### 2) Inspect terminals and clamps for corrosion/looseness

Corrosion at the post-to-clamp interface can create major voltage drop and no-start symptoms.

> High-risk quote: "This can be a simple problem of a bad connection at the battery terminals... corrosion ... can cause a connection problem." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/everything-goes-dead-when-engine-is-cranked

### 3) Disconnect and clean safely

- Disconnect negative, then positive.
- Clean corrosion with baking soda + water and a brush.
- Avoid letting cleaning mixture enter battery vents/caps.

> High-risk quote: "To get rid of battery terminal corrosion, mix baking soda and distilled water, dip a brush in the mixture, and then brush away the blue-green foam carefully." https://www.interstatebatteries.com/blog/how-to-clean-battery-corrosion

### 4) Dry and inspect metal contact surfaces

After neutralizing and cleaning, dry completely and inspect for:
- pitted/damaged clamp bores,
- cracked terminals,
- cable corrosion creeping under insulation.

Replace damaged hardware rather than forcing a poor connection.

### 5) Reassemble and tighten correctly

- Reconnect positive first, then negative.
- Tighten securely without over-tightening small clamp hardware.
- Apply terminal protectant (optional) after final fitment.

### 6) Function check (basic load behavior)

Use a simple practical check:
- Observe headlight brightness before and during crank.
- Severe dimming/clicking under crank suggests weak battery or high resistance.

> High-risk quote: "If the headlights dim way down and go out... the battery has failed and it must be replaced." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/car-battery-load-test

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- This guide avoids publishing one universal terminal clamp torque value.
- Clamp hardware design varies by battery/terminal style; tighten to secure contact without thread damage.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Engine cranks normally without abnormal dimming/clicking.
- Terminals remain cool and secure after a short drive.
- Recheck for renewed corrosion after several days.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Publicly accessible sources in this run align well on cleaning chemistry/safety and practical load symptoms.
- A single NB-specific OEM battery terminal torque value and one universal battery group/CCA table were not reliably extractable in this environment; confirm final replacement spec by year/market manual and installed battery tray/cable setup.
