## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Poor headlight aim can severely reduce night visibility and increase glare for oncoming traffic.

> Warning: Perform aiming on level ground and secure the vehicle so it cannot roll during setup. https://www.popularmechanics.com/cars/a63828996/how-to-adjust-headlights/

> High-risk quote: "if you're using the shine-on-a-wall method, aiming a low beam just 2.3 cm (0.9 inch) lower than it should be cuts 26 m (85 feet) off your seeing distance at night". https://www.danielsternlighting.com/tech/aim/aim.html

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era factory manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact adjuster locations, beam-spec targets, and market-specific legal requirements must be verified by year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Tape measure
- Painter’s tape/chalk for wall marks
- Screwdriver/socket for headlight adjusters (varies by lamp unit)
- Tire pressure gauge

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Usually none for adjustment-only work
- Replacement adjuster hardware if stripped/broken

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Headlamp internals and adjuster condition vary widely with age; inspect for stripped adjusters before aiming.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- OEM, NBFL, and aftermarket projector/housing swaps are common; verify your actual housing style and adjuster function first.

> Community quote: r/Miata search results include NB-specific reports like "Broken NB headlight adjuster" and "Does NB1 headlight adjustment work with NB2 headlights?" showing hardware-variant issues are common. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=nb%20headlight%20adjustment&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Pre-aim vehicle setup

- Set tire pressures correctly.
- Remove unusual cargo weight.
- Use typical fuel load (about half tank is commonly cited in aiming guides).
- Bounce each corner of the car lightly to settle suspension.

### 2) Prepare aiming surface

- Park on level ground facing a flat wall.
- Mark headlamp centerlines on the wall at near distance.
- Back vehicle to your chosen measured distance (commonly 25 ft / 7.6 m unless your manual specifies otherwise).

### 3) Mark target reference lines

- Create a horizontal reference line at lamp center height.
- Create centerline marks for each lamp.
- Add a lower target line per your manual/regional method.

### 4) Adjust low beams first

- Use vertical and horizontal adjusters in small increments.
- Recheck both sides after every small change.
- Keep left-right balance and avoid excessive upward aim.

### 5) Verify high-beam relationship

High beams often track low-beam mechanical alignment; check both after low-beam setting.

### 6) Diagnose if adjustment fails

If one side won’t adjust or drops suddenly:
- inspect stripped adjuster gears/screws,
- inspect broken internal reflector mounts,
- inspect non-matching aftermarket housing parts.

> Community confirmation quote: old.reddit NB search context includes repeated reports of one-side non-responsive adjusters and low-beam aim collapse, consistent with worn adjuster hardware. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=nb+headlight+aim&restrict_sr=on

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Adjuster fasteners and any disturbed mounting hardware: **year/VIN FSM only**

No universal publish-safe NB-wide numeric aim table is provided due market/legal differences and housing variations.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Good forward visibility without obvious foreground over-brightness
- No frequent oncoming-driver flashing complaints
- Beam cutoffs appear even and stable side-to-side
- Recheck aim after any front suspension/headlamp housing work

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources conflict on exact wall-distance and drop values by region/manufacturer.
- Community evidence was partly search/snippet level.
- Therefore this guide provides a conservative method and defers final numeric/legal targets to year/VIN documentation and local regulations.
