## Before You Start / Safety

- Ignition OFF before pulling/installing fuses.
- Never bridge contacts with metal tools.
- Replace only with the **same amperage and fuse type**.

## Required Tools

- Fuse puller (or insulated needle-nose pliers)
- Flashlight
- Multimeter or test light (recommended)
- Spare blade fuses: 7.5 A, 10 A, 15 A, 20 A, 30 A

## Where the fuse boxes are on NB

Typical NB layout:
- **Engine bay fuse/relay box:** left side of bay near battery area/firewall side
- **Cabin fuse panel:** driver footwell lower dash area (behind trim cover)

Always confirm with the diagram inside your own fuse-box cover and year-specific manual.

## Fast blade-fuse color check (backup only)

Read the printed amp number first; color is only a cross-check:
- Brown 7.5 A
- Red 10 A
- Blue 15 A
- Yellow 20 A
- Green 30 A

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm the exact failed function

Write down precisely what fails (e.g., left low beam, horn, cabin blower speed 1-2-3, etc.).
This reduces random fuse pulling.

### 2) Find the circuit fuse from cover legend/manual

Use your cover chart and manual page for your market/year. Some slot assignments vary by year/options.

### 3) Pull and inspect suspect fuse

Remove fuse and inspect the element:
- intact bridge = likely okay,
- broken/blackened bridge = blown.

### 4) Verify with a meter/test light (best practice)

With circuit energized as needed:
- power on both test points = fuse likely good,
- power on one side only = blown fuse.

### 5) Install exact replacement

Install same blade size and same amperage.

**Do not up-rate** (e.g., replacing 15 A with 20 A). Over-fusing can overheat wiring.

### 6) Re-test and decide next action

If the new fuse blows again quickly:
- stop replacing repeatedly,
- inspect downstream load/wiring for short,
- unplug suspected component and retest.

## Quick repeat-failure triage

If fuse blows instantly on key ON:
- suspect short to ground on supply side/component.

If fuse blows only when activating one function:
- suspect that actuator/device (motor, lamp holder, relay output).

If fuse survives static but blows over bumps:
- suspect harness chafe/intermittent contact.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Failed function restored
- Fuse remains intact after 2-3 operation cycles
- No burning smell, heat discoloration, or repeat trip
