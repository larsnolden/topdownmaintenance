## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)**.

Most headlight bulb failures are simple, but poor handling or bad connector condition can cause rapid repeat burnout.

- Turn lights and ignition OFF.
- Let lamp housing cool before touching rear cap or bulb.
- Wear clean gloves; never touch halogen glass with bare fingers.

## Required Tools

- Nitrile gloves
- Small trim tool/flat screwdriver (if cap clip is tight)
- Lint-free cloth + isopropyl alcohol (for accidental glass contact cleanup)
- Flashlight

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct replacement bulb for your housing
- Optional connector/pigtail repair kit if plug is heat-damaged

### Practical bulb-selection rules

1. Match the exact bulb code from old bulb and/or year/VIN documentation.
2. Match OEM wattage (avoid over-watt bulbs in stock wiring/housings).
3. Replace left and right bulbs as a pair for color/brightness consistency.

## Where to access the bulb on NB

Open the hood and stand above the headlight from engine-bay side.

At the rear of each headlamp you will usually find:
- an electrical plug on bulb base,
- a rubber/plastic dust cover,
- a spring clip or retainer securing the bulb.

Visual cue: the bulb sits in the center of the rear housing; parking-light sockets are smaller and usually offset.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Verify the failed side and baseline function

Before disassembly, confirm:
- low beam status,
- high beam status,
- opposite side function.

If both sides are out, check fuses/relays first before replacing bulbs blindly.

### 2) Open rear housing carefully

- Remove rear dust cap (pull type or twist type depending housing).
- Inspect inside cap/housing for moisture, white haze, or rust marks.

If condensation is present, plan seal/cap correction or failure may repeat.

### 3) Disconnect and release bulb

- Pull connector straight back (no side-to-side prying on terminals).
- Release spring clip/retainer.
- Remove bulb straight out while noting tab orientation.

### 4) Inspect connector condition before installing new bulb

Check for:
- browned or melted plug body,
- loose terminal grip,
- green corrosion on terminals,
- brittle insulation near plug.

If damaged, repair connector first; a new bulb alone may fail quickly.

### 5) Install new bulb correctly

- Hold by metal/plastic base only.
- Align tabs exactly and seat bulb fully flush.
- Lock retainer securely.
- Reconnect plug firmly.
- Reinstall dust cap fully around its sealing lip.

If bulb glass was touched, clean with alcohol and lint-free cloth before final install.

### 6) Test and quick beam sanity check

- Test low/high beam on both sides.
- Check color and brightness match.
- Do a short wall check at night/dusk for obvious mis-aim.

If beam pattern shifted, continue with formal headlight-aim procedure.

## Practical post-replacement checks

After 5-10 minutes of lamp operation:
- no burning-plastic smell at rear connector,
- no flicker when lightly tapping housing/loom,
- no immediate condensation build-up behind lens.
