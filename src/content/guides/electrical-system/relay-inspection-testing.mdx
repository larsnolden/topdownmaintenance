## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Relays are often blamed too early. Confirm power supply, grounds, fuse integrity, and load-side wiring before replacing relays blindly.

> Warning: Disable ignition and secure vehicle before probing energized circuits. https://www.autozone.com/diy

> High-risk quote: Quick relay checks should verify both coil-side control and load-side output; a click alone does not prove full function. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-test-an-automotive-relay

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so relay-box layout and pin designations must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before test-point probing. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Digital multimeter
- Test light
- Relay puller/needle-nose pliers
- Known-good relay of matching pin layout (for swap test)
- Wiring diagram/FSM reference

### Relay-box location orientation (NB)

NB cars commonly have relay/fuse blocks in the engine-bay fuse/relay box area; some circuits may also involve interior fuse/relay locations depending on market/equipment.

Before probing:
- confirm exact box and cavity from year/VIN diagram,
- identify pin numbers on relay base/socket (not just terminal shape).

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct replacement relay(s) only after testing confirms fault

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Relay locations and labeling can vary by market and equipment package.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: ABS/AC/fan/fuel circuits may use different relay box layouts than NB1 or swapped harnesses.

Use year/VIN relay maps to avoid misidentifying similarly shaped relays across adjacent circuits.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptom and affected subsystem

Identify which system fails:
- no-start/fuel pump prime issues,
- cooling fan not triggering,
- intermittent main-power behavior.

### 2) Check fuses and feed voltage first

Before touching relay:
- verify associated fuses,
- verify battery/system voltage,
- verify ground condition.

### 3) Relay identification sanity check

Use FSM year/VIN diagram to confirm:
- relay location,
- pin numbering,
- circuit function.

Do not trust universal internet relay maps for NB variants.

### 4) Quick functional tests

- Listen/feel for relay actuation (if accessible).
- Test coil-side control voltage and ground when command is present.
- Test load-side output voltage to downstream circuit.

Practical logic:
- click present + no load output => possible contact failure/high resistance,
- no click + proper coil command missing => upstream command/power/ground issue,
- output present + component still inoperative => downstream wiring/load fault.

### 5) Swap test with identical known-good relay

If pinout/current rating are identical, swap with a non-critical circuit relay briefly.

Swap-test checklist:
- same pin layout,
- same relay type (normally open vs changeover where applicable),
- same or higher OEM-equivalent current rating.

If symptom moves with relay, relay fault is likely.

### 6) Confirm downstream component before replacement

A good relay cannot fix:
- failed pump/fan motor,
- corroded connectors,
- broken wires,
- bad grounds.

### 7) Final verification

After repair/replacement:
- verify repeatable circuit operation across temperature cycles,
- recheck for heat at relay/socket,
- confirm no intermittent dropouts.

Relay replacement should only follow proof of failed relay function, not be used as first-line fault isolation.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Target circuit operates reliably (pump/fan/main feed)
- No relay/socket overheating after operation
- No repeat intermittent fault after short drive/idle cycles
