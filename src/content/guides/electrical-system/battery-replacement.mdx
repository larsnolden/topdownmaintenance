## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Battery swaps are straightforward, but incorrect size/terminal orientation/hold-down can cause shorts, acid leakage risk, or intermittent electrical faults.

> Warning: Wear eye and hand protection when handling batteries and terminals. https://www.autozone.com/diy/battery/how-to-replace-a-car-battery

> High-risk quote: Disconnect the **negative terminal first** and reconnect it last to reduce short-circuit risk. https://www.autozone.com/diy/battery/how-to-replace-a-car-battery

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so battery specification, hold-down details, and any venting requirements must be confirmed by exact year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- 10 mm wrench/socket (typical terminal size)
- Socket/ratchet for hold-down hardware
- Battery terminal cleaner/brush
- Battery-safe terminal protectant
- Gloves and safety glasses

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct battery size/spec for your NB year/market
- Replacement terminal clamp or hold-down hardware if damaged/corroded
- Optional anti-corrosion pads

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm battery fitment before removal

Check:
- battery footprint/group size,
- terminal polarity orientation,
- hold-down compatibility,
- cable reach without strain.

### 2) Remove old battery safely

- Ignition off, key out.
- Disconnect **negative** terminal first.
- Disconnect positive terminal.
- Remove hold-down bracket.
- Lift battery out upright.

### 3) Clean and inspect tray/cables

- Clean tray and neutralize corrosion residue as appropriate.
- Inspect cables for cracked insulation or heat damage.
- Clean terminal clamps to bright contact surfaces.

### 4) Install new battery

- Place battery with correct orientation.
- Reinstall hold-down; battery must not move in tray.
- Connect positive terminal first, then negative terminal.
- Confirm clamp tightness without over-deforming terminal posts.

### 5) Venting and restraint checks

Where applicable, ensure vent provisions and restraint hardware are correct per your battery style and FSM guidance.

### 6) Post-swap validation

- Start engine and verify strong crank.
- Check charging voltage and warning lamps.
- Recheck terminal tightness after short drive.

> Community confirmation quote: old.reddit NB battery-replacement search context frequently points owners to Miata.net/MX5Nutz for exact tray/hold-down fitment when using non-stock battery sizes. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=nb+battery+replacement&restrict_sr=on
