## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)**.

A battery swap is easy when done cleanly, but wrong fitment or poor hold-down can cause intermittent electrical faults and premature battery failure.

- Wear eye and hand protection.
- Remove metal jewelry before terminal work.
- Keep tools clear of body metal when working at positive terminal.

## Required Tools

- 10 mm wrench/socket (typical terminal clamp size)
- Socket/ratchet for hold-down hardware
- Terminal brush/cleaner
- Battery terminal protectant (optional)
- Gloves + safety glasses

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct battery for your NB tray and cable orientation
- Replacement clamp/hold-down hardware if corroded or stripped
- Optional anti-corrosion pads

### Practical battery selection checklist

Confirm all four before purchase:
1. **Case size** fits tray and hold-down.
2. **Terminal orientation** matches cable length naturally.
3. **Capacity** (Ah/CCA) is at least OEM-equivalent for your climate/use.
4. **Battery type/venting** is compatible with your installed location setup.

If choosing between options, prioritize correct fit + secure hold-down over marketing CCA numbers.

## Where the battery is on NB

Battery location can differ by market/spec and prior modifications.

Common NB layouts you may encounter:
- in-trunk location on one side behind trim panel, or
- engine-bay placement on converted/modified cars.

Before disconnecting anything:
- locate battery physically,
- photograph cable routing and hold-down arrangement,
- note any vent tube connection.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Pre-removal checks

- Ignition OFF, key out.
- Verify radio/security presets if needed.
- Confirm replacement battery orientation against installed one.

### 2) Disconnect in correct order

1. Disconnect **negative (-) terminal first**.
2. Disconnect positive (+) terminal.
3. Remove hold-down bracket.
4. Lift battery out upright.

Never tilt heavily if battery may vent electrolyte.

### 3) Clean and inspect tray/cables

- Clean tray and remove corrosion debris.
- Inspect cable insulation near clamps for cracks/hardening.
- Clean clamp interiors to bright metal contact.
- Check ground cable connection points for corrosion.

### 4) Install replacement battery

- Set battery in tray with correct polarity orientation.
- Install hold-down so battery cannot shift.
- Connect positive (+) first, then negative (-).
- Tighten clamps until secure; avoid crushing terminal posts.

### 5) Physical security check (must pass)

With both clamps tight:
- push battery by hand in all directions.
- If it moves, correct hold-down fitment before driving.

### 6) Post-install electrical validation

- Start engine and confirm strong crank.
- Confirm no battery warning lamp.
- Measure charging voltage at battery terminals:
  - typical running range around **13.5-14.8 V** depending load/temperature.

### 7) Short-drive recheck

After 10-20 minute drive:
- recheck terminal tightness,
- confirm no hot-terminal smell,
- confirm battery remains fully restrained.

## Practical mistakes to avoid

- Disconnecting positive first (higher short risk).
- Installing reversed orientation and stressing cables to reach.
- Reusing damaged hold-down hardware.
- Leaving trunk/engine-bay venting/seals incorrectly routed where applicable.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Reliable start hot and cold
- No terminal heating/arcing signs
- Battery warning lamp stays off
- Battery fixed securely with zero tray movement
