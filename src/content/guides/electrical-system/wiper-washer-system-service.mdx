## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Wiper/washer faults are safety-relevant because poor visibility can make driving hazardous in rain/spray conditions.

> Warning: Keep hands clear of wiper linkage and blades during powered tests. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/windshield-washer-not-working

> High-risk quote: A no-spray symptom can come from low fluid, clogged nozzles/hoses, blown fuse, or failed pump—verify each path before replacing parts. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/windshield-washer-not-working

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era factory manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact fuse/relay locations, harness routing, and washer circuit details must be confirmed by year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Trim-safe pry tool/small screwdriver
- Multimeter or test light
- Needle/probe for nozzle clearing (gently used)
- Small hose clamp or pinch tool (optional)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Windshield washer fluid suitable for climate
- Replacement pump/nozzle/hose segments as needed
- Replacement grommet/seal if pump interface leaks

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Washer hose routing and nozzle style can differ by market and prior repairs.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: aftermarket hood/nozzle hardware may alter spray pattern and service access.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptom type

Differentiate:
- wipers move but no spray,
- spray weak/uneven,
- pump runs but no fluid at nozzles,
- no pump sound at all.

### 2) Basic supply checks first

- Verify washer reservoir fluid level and condition.
- Check for frozen fluid (seasonal).
- Confirm fuse state before touching pump wiring.

### 3) Pump operation check

- Command washer and listen for pump.
- If silent, check voltage/ground at pump connector during switch command.
- If powered but silent, pump is likely failed.

### 4) Hose and leak-path inspection

- Inspect hoses from reservoir to nozzles for splits/disconnects.
- Look for fluid leak under hood/tray area during activation.
- Re-seat or replace brittle hose sections.

### 5) Nozzle inspection and aiming

- Clear nozzle deposits gently.
- Aim stream to hit windshield in useful sweep area (not over-roof or below blade path).
- Re-test with engine running and vehicle level.

### 6) Wiper functional check (paired system validation)

- Verify blade condition and wipe quality.
- Confirm washers and wipers operate together as expected.
- If wash works but wipe is poor, service blades/arms/linkage separately.

> Community confirmation quote: old.reddit NB washer-pump search context repeatedly points owners to inspect disconnected hoses/nozzle blockage before replacing motors or stalk switches. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=nb+windshield+washer+pump&restrict_sr=on

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

No major torque specs apply for basic washer/nozzle service.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Pump runs reliably on command
- Spray pattern reaches correct windshield area
- No fluid leaks along hose path or pump seal
- Wipers clear fluid effectively without chatter/streaking
