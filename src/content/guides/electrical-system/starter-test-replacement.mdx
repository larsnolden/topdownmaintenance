## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

A no-crank condition is not always a bad starter. Battery state, cable condition, grounds, relay/control wiring, and ignition-switch output can all mimic starter failure.

> Warning: "Make sure the parking brake is set" before under-car starter testing work. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/starter-not-working-repair

> High-risk quote: "use a test light to see if the starter solenoid is getting 12 volts" in crank mode before condemning the starter itself. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/starter-not-working-repair

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact starter circuit tests, connector pinouts, and mounting torque specs must be confirmed by year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptom type

Differentiate:
- **No-crank** (starter does not rotate engine),
- **Crank-no-start** (starter turns engine but engine does not fire).

This guide is for **no-crank** diagnosis/replacement.

### 2) Battery and cable baseline first

- Verify battery voltage/state of charge.
- Inspect positive and ground cable connections.
- Check for corrosion or heat-damaged cable ends.

### 3) Starter control-circuit test

At starter trigger wire, command crank and verify whether control voltage appears.

If no trigger voltage, diagnose upstream controls (relay, switch, immobilizer/clutch/neutral inputs as applicable) before replacing starter.

### 4) Main feed and voltage-drop checks

- Confirm constant battery feed at starter main terminal.
- Check voltage drop on positive and negative paths during crank command.

If supply drops excessively, repair battery/cable/ground faults first.

### 5) Starter verdict and replacement

If trigger and feed are correct but starter does not crank reliably, starter internal fault is likely.

- Disconnect battery.
- Remove starter in FSM sequence.
- Install replacement unit and reconnect wiring securely.
- Torque mounting hardware to FSM values.

### 6) Post-install verification

- Confirm consistent crank speed hot/cold.
- Verify no abnormal starter noise.
- Recheck battery/charging condition after repair.

> Community confirmation quote: old.reddit NB starter replacement search context repeatedly shows users finding low trigger voltage/ground issues before final starter replacement decisions. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=nb+starter+replacement&restrict_sr=on

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Engine cranks immediately and consistently
- No click-only/no-crank recurrence
- No excessive voltage drop during cranking
- No hot-start failure after full warm-up
