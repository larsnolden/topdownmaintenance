## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Sway bar bushing service is straightforward, but incorrect bushing size or bracket fit can cause persistent clunks and handling inconsistency.

> Warning: "Use jack stands to support the vehicle if needed." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-a-brake-master-cylinder

> High-risk quote: "Use proper safety gear and follow all safety precautions." https://www.autozone.com/diy

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era factory manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so sway bar bracket torque values and installation orientation must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before final tightening. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Socket/wrench set
- Torque wrench
- Vernier caliper (preferred) or accurate diameter gauge for bar OD
- Silicone-safe bushing lubricant (if bushing manufacturer specifies)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct sway bar bushings for measured bar diameter
- Bracket hardware if corroded/damaged
- Optional heavy-duty brackets when applicable to upgraded bars/setups

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Bar diameters can vary by trim/package and prior modifications.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: many cars have aftermarket bars or mixed NA/NB parts; measure before ordering.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm bushing-related symptoms

Typical signs:
- low-speed front/rear clunk over small bumps,
- dry-rubber squeak,
- visible bushing split/compression set,
- sway bar movement inside bushing bracket.

Rule out sway bar end links first.

### 2) Measure sway bar diameter before ordering/installation

Do **not** assume diameter by model year alone.

Measure bar OD at a clean straight section near bushing seat; match to bushing ID exactly.

### 3) Lift/support and remove brackets

- Raise vehicle safely.
- Support bar as needed to reduce bracket tension.
- Remove bracket bolts evenly and note bushing split orientation.

### 4) Inspect bar contact surfaces and brackets

- Clean bar bushing seat area.
- Check for rust pitting/grooves where bushing rides.
- Inspect bracket deformation/cracks.

Replace damaged brackets or heavily corroded contact areas as needed.

### 5) Install new bushings and brackets

- Fit bushings with split orientation as specified by kit/FSM.
- Apply only approved lubricant type/amount.
- Reinstall brackets and hand-start bolts to avoid cross-threading.

### 6) Final torque and verification

- Torque bracket hardware to FSM values.
- Recheck sway bar centering.
- Road test for clunk/squeak and confirm stable turn-in feel.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Sway bar bracket bolt torques: **year/VIN FSM only**
- Any upgraded-bracket hardware specs: **manufacturer + FSM cross-check**

No universal publish-safe NB-wide torque table is provided.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No clunk or squeak from serviced axle over low-speed bumps
- Sway bar remains centered with no bracket migration
- No new steering pull or abnormal handling response
