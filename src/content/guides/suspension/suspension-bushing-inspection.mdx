## Before You Start / Safety

- Park on a flat surface and chock wheels.
- If lifting the car, use secure stands before applying pry force.
- Do not place hands between control arms and chassis during loaded/unloaded checks.

> Warning: "Make sure your car is jacked securely. The vehicle must be on frame stands when jacked up and you are working on the vehicle." https://www.wikihow.com/Check-Ball-Joints

## Required Tools

- Jack and axle stands
- Flashlight
- Pry bar
- Marker/chalk (to mark suspect bushings)
- Gloves and eye protection

## Required Parts / Fluids

Inspection only:
- No parts required

If faults are found:
- Correct NB1/NB2 bushing set(s) for affected arms/locations
- New alignment hardware where required

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB bushing condition and replacement strategy often depends on mileage, age, climate, and prior suspension work.
- Always confirm exact bushing part numbers by VIN/year and suspension configuration.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "That mileage should not need bushings...the OE last a lot longer than people think" https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=683815

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Symptom pre-check

Record complaints before lifting:
- vague rear tracking,
- clunks over bumps,
- alignment drift,
- visible wheel deflection under load.

### 2) Visual inspection of all accessible bushings

At each corner, inspect control-arm and related suspension bushings for:
- cracking/perishing,
- tearing/splitting,
- sleeve separation,
- obvious distortion.

Use a consistent left-vs-right comparison so asymmetry is easier to spot.

> High-risk quote: "They tend to wear with age, heat and stress, becoming loose and cracking." https://www.moogparts.com/en-eu/support/the-problem-solver/technical-support/educational-articles/front-end-assembly-check.html

### 3) Movement check with pry bar

Apply controlled leverage near the bushing housing (not on fragile components). Excessive movement, audible knock, or visible separation indicates replacement-level wear.

### 4) Decide selective vs full replacement scope

When only one or two locations are clearly failed, selective replacement may be reasonable. If multiple bushings are similarly aged/cracked, a broader refresh can reduce repeat alignments and labor duplication.

### 5) Preload and tightening principle (if any bushings are replaced)

Rubber bushings must be final-tightened at normal ride height to avoid built-in twist/preload.

> Community confirmation quote: "Remember when replacing your bushings to tighten the wishbone/control arm bolts with the car’s weight resting on the wheels so there is no preload on the rubber at rest!" https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=627815

### 6) Plan alignment after bushing work

Any removed control arm or eccentric hardware can affect alignment. Schedule an alignment check after replacement work.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- This inspection guide does not publish a universal torque table.
- Bushing carrier/control-arm fastener values vary by location and year; confirm in NB-specific workshop manual data before final tightening.

## Verification / Post-service checks

After inspection (or after any replacement work):
- Confirm all hardware is present and seated.
- Re-test for clunk/play at low speed.
- Verify steering self-centering and straight tracking.
- Perform wheel alignment check if suspension fasteners were loosened.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Publicly accessible sources are consistent on inspection method (visual + movement + symptom correlation) and ride-height tightening requirements.
- A single extractable NB-wide bushing torque matrix was not reliably available in this environment; final values should come from year/VIN-specific FSM sections.
