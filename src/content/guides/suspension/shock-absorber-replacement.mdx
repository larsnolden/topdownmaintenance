## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Shock replacement can involve spring preload energy. If your method requires spring removal, use proper spring-compressor technique and PPE.

> Warning: "When replacing struts, a spring compressor is needed to remove the spring from the strut." https://www.autozone.com/diy

> High-risk quote: "Do one side at a time and use the opposite side as a reference." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-a-brake-master-cylinder

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era factory manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so suspension fastener torques and ride-height-related tightening sequence must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before final torque. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Socket/wrench set, breaker bar
- Torque wrench
- Penetrant and rust-safe cleanup tools
- Spring compressor (if disassembling spring/damper assembly)
- Paint marker for baseline hardware orientation

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct shock absorbers for NB axle position and package
- Top mounts/bump stops/dust boots if worn
- Replacement self-locking nuts/bolts where required

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Verify installed suspension package before ordering; many cars have mixed components.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Sport/Bilstein package differences are common discussion points; verify actual installed dampers and mounts before parts purchase.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm damper replacement need

Use bounce/leak/handling symptoms plus visual inspection (oil seepage, damaged shaft, degraded bushings).

Replace in axle pairs (both fronts or both rears minimum) to keep damping balance predictable.

### 2) Pre-loosen and safe support

- Loosen wheel nuts on ground.
- Lift and support vehicle safely.
- Remove wheel and inspect adjacent bushings/end links while accessible.

### 3) Remove shock assembly per position

- Mark orientation and hardware stack order.
- Support suspension arm to control droop.
- Remove lower then upper attachments in FSM-guided sequence.

### 4) Spring-transfer step (if required)

If your replacement plan requires moving spring/top mount to new damper:
- compress spring evenly with approved compressor,
- never point stored spring energy toward body/face,
- inspect mount bearing, bump stop, and isolators before reuse.

### 5) Install new shocks and related hardware

- Assemble in correct orientation.
- Start all fasteners by hand.
- Final torque at FSM-specified ride-height condition where required.

### 6) Post-install checks

- Recheck wheel clearance and brake hose routing.
- Road test for clunks, rebound control, and steering return.
- Schedule alignment check after suspension disturbance.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Upper/lower shock fastener torques: **year/VIN FSM only**

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No fluid leaks or abnormal noise from new dampers
- Stable rebound behavior over bumps
- No new pull/steering instability
- Fastener recheck after short bedding-in interval
