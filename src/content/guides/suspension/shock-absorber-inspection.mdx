## Before You Start / Safety

- Park on level ground, engage the handbrake, and chock wheels.
- If lifting the car, support it with stands before touching suspension components.
- Avoid placing fingers between suspension parts while jouncing the body.

> Warning: "Park the vehicle on a dry, flat surface and install wheel chocks... Use secure jack points for the jack and jack stands." https://www.autozone.com/diy/brakes/how-to-replace-brake-pads-and-rotors

## Required Tools

- Flashlight or inspection lamp
- Clean rag/paper towel
- Jack and axle stands (if inspecting with wheel removed)
- Pry bar (optional, for bushing play checks)
- Tire-pressure gauge (for symptom triage consistency)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- No parts required for inspection-only work
- If faults are found: replacement shocks, bump stops, dust boots, and bushings as needed

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 and NB2 can use different damper/spring packages depending on trim and market.
- Confirm fitment by VIN and existing hardware (especially if your car was previously modified).

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "If you need new shocks and don’t want to lower your car, this is a great choice." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=799338

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Road-symptom triage (before touching tools)

On a familiar route, note whether the car:
- bounces excessively after bumps,
- feels unstable in quick direction changes,
- dives/squats more than expected under braking/acceleration.

> High-risk quote: "You’ll notice increased stopping distances, reduced ability to maneuver the vehicle safely..." https://www.autozone.com/diy/shocks-and-struts/symptoms-of-bad-shocks-and-struts

### 2) Static bounce check

At each corner, press down firmly and release. The body should settle quickly rather than oscillate repeatedly.

### 3) Visual damper check

Inspect each shock body and shaft area:
- oil leakage/wetness,
- shaft damage/corrosion,
- dented bodies,
- torn dust boots.

AutoZone and community guidance both treat visible leakage or physical damage as replacement-level findings.

### 4) Mount and bushing inspection

Check upper/lower shock mounts and bushings for:
- cracking,
- crushing,
- separation,
- abnormal movement/clunk clues.

### 5) Broader suspension context check

If symptoms remain ambiguous, inspect adjacent components (control arm joints/bushings, links, tires, alignment condition) so shock diagnosis is not made in isolation.

> Community confirmation quote: "Remember when replacing your bushings to tighten the wishbone/control arm bolts with the car’s weight resting on the wheels so there is no preload on the rubber at rest!" https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=627815

### 6) Replacement threshold decision

Plan shock replacement when you have any of the following:
- clear leakage or damage,
- persistent uncontrolled bounce,
- handling degradation not explained by tire pressure/alignment/other wear items.

Replace in axle pairs to keep damping balanced left-to-right.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Inspection stage has no fluid capacity values.
- For replacement torque values (upper/lower fasteners), use NB year- and trim-specific factory manual data.

## Verification / Post-service checks

After inspection (or replacement):
- Confirm no tool/contact damage and all hardware is present.
- Road-test at low/moderate speed first.
- Verify straight-line stability and predictable damping over uneven surfaces.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Publicly accessible sources in this environment are stronger on symptom/inspection logic than on one complete NB-wide torque table for all shock packages.
- Final replacement torque numbers should be pulled from VIN/year-specific NB workshop documentation.
