## Before You Start / Safety

- Park on level ground, chock rear wheels, and use axle stands if the front is lifted.
- Keep hands clear of pinch points when levering suspension components.
- Any suspected severe play should be treated as safety-critical and inspected before further road use.

> Warning: "Make sure your car is jacked securely. The vehicle must be on frame stands when jacked up and you are working on the vehicle." https://www.wikihow.com/Check-Ball-Joints

## Required Tools

- Jack and axle stands
- Pry bar
- Flashlight
- Gloves and eye protection
- Helper (recommended for wheel-play checks)

## Required Parts / Fluids

Inspection-only:
- No replacement parts required

If faults are found:
- Ball-joint dust boots (where serviceable)
- Upper control arm / ball joint assembly (as required)
- Cotter pins and locking hardware per service manual

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB upper-joint parts and boot service choices can vary by year and supplier listings; verify fitment by VIN and removed-part dimensions.
- Community reports show owners encountering year-listing differences and choosing either boot service or arm replacement depending on joint condition.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "I see the boots are torn on Emma's upper ball joints, so it's just a matter of time before replacing." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=785571

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Symptom pre-check (road test + visual)

Before lifting the car, note clunks, wandering, vague steering response, or irregular front tire wear. Then do a visual check for split boots and grease leakage.

### 2) Lift and unload correctly

Raise and support the front end so you can check for joint movement without unsafe loading. Follow model-appropriate lifting points and ensure stable stand placement.

### 3) Wheel play checks

Check vertical and radial play at the wheel while observing joint movement at the knuckle/control-arm area.

> High-risk quote: "Read the dial indicator and check against the manufacturer’s specifications. If the movement is beyond specifications, replace the ball joint." https://www.wikihow.com/Check-Ball-Joints

### 4) Pry-bar confirmation

Use a pry bar carefully to load/unload the joint and confirm whether movement is in the joint itself rather than wheel bearing, bushing, or tie-rod play.

### 5) Boot-only vs replacement decision

- If boot is damaged but joint remains tight/smooth, boot replacement may be considered.
- If looseness, roughness, or clear play is present, move to full component replacement.

> Community confirmation quote: "If the joints are still OK, get new boots from Mazda." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=785571

> Community confirmation quote: "Same kind of money for each choice. I would go with a new upper control arm." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=791346

### 6) Post-inspection risk check

If any uncertain play remains, do not assume it is harmless. Re-check with a second method (helper + pry bar + repeat wheel-play test) and compare against FSM limits before returning the car to normal use.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- This guide is inspection-focused; no universal torque/capacity values are published here.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Confirm all disturbed fasteners are resecured.
- Recheck wheel movement after reassembly.
- Perform cautious low-speed road test and listen for clunk/knock recurrence.
