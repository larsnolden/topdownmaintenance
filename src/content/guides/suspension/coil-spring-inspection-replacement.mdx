## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Coil springs store high energy. Incorrect compression/removal can cause severe injury.

> Warning: "When replacing struts, a spring compressor is needed to remove the spring from the strut." https://www.autozone.com/diy

> High-risk quote: "Use proper safety gear and follow all safety precautions." https://www.autozone.com/diy

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era factory manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so spring free-length limits, orientation notes, and torque values must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before final assembly. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Socket/wrench set, breaker bar
- Torque wrench
- Approved spring compressor (matched to spring diameter)
- Paint marker for orientation marks
- PPE (eye/hand protection)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct front/rear coil springs for your NB configuration
- New top mount insulators/bearings if worn
- Bump stops/dust boots if degraded
- New self-locking nuts/bolts where required

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Verify stock spring type and any previous lowering-spring changes before ordering.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Package differences and prior swaps are common; confirm actual installed spring/shock combination first.

> Community quote: r/Miata NB spring-replacement search repeatedly surfaces mixed-hardware and compatibility questions (stock vs lowering setups). https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=nb%20coil%20spring%20replacement&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm spring condition and symptom

Look for:
- broken coils (often at lower end),
- heavy corrosion/pitting,
- sagging ride height,
- side-to-side height mismatch,
- spring-seat noise.

### 2) Pre-service measurements

Record baseline fender-to-hub (or FSM-specified) ride-height values at all four corners.

This helps verify post-install settlement and identify unrelated suspension issues.

### 3) Remove damper/spring assembly

- Support vehicle safely and remove wheel.
- Remove relevant links/fasteners per FSM.
- Mark assembly orientation before disassembly.

### 4) Compress spring safely

- Install compressor evenly on opposite sides.
- Compress incrementally and symmetrically.
- Keep body/face clear of spring axis.

> High-risk quote: "Do I have to use spring compressors?" is a recurring Miata community safety question; treat compressor use as mandatory when preload remains. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=nb%20spring%20compressor&restrict_sr=1

### 5) Inspect related hardware during disassembly

- Top mount bearing/isolator condition
- Bump stop integrity
- Damper shaft condition and leaks
- Spring seat corrosion or deformation

Replace worn companion parts now to avoid repeat labor.

### 6) Install replacement spring(s)

- Match spring orientation to seat indexing points.
- Reassemble with correct stack order.
- Replace in axle pairs at minimum (both fronts or both rears).

### 7) Final tightening and post-install checks

- Torque fasteners per FSM, including any ride-height-loaded steps.
- Reinstall wheels and lower vehicle.
- Bounce/settle suspension, then recheck ride height.
- Perform alignment check after spring replacement.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Spring/damper top nut, lower mounting bolts, and related link torques: **year/VIN FSM only**
- Ride-height-loaded tightening requirements: **year/VIN FSM only**

No universal publish-safe NB spring replacement torque table is provided.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No spring-seat clunk or bind noises
- Left/right ride height is within expected tolerance after settling
- Stable steering return and no abnormal pull
- Alignment confirmed after suspension work
