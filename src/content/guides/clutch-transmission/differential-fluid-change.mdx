## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)**.

Differential service is simple but critical: wrong fluid type or incorrect fill level can cause noise, poor LSD behavior, and accelerated wear.

> Warning: Always loosen the **fill plug first** before draining; if the fill plug is seized and you drain first, the car may be left undrivable. https://www.autozone.com/diy

> High-risk quote: Gear oil level and specification directly affect differential durability; low level from leaks or service errors can lead to serious damage. https://www.aa1car.com/library/oil_leaks.htm

> Manual-reference quote: NB FSM sets are split by year groups (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact fluid specification/capacity and plug torque must be confirmed by year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Jack/stands or lift
- Correct socket/hex for diff fill/drain plugs
- Drain pan
- Fluid pump for refill
- Torque wrench

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Differential oil (spec per year/VIN and differential type)
- New plug sealing washers if required
- LSD-appropriate oil/friction strategy as required by your specific differential

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Open vs LSD differential fitment varies by market/trim.
- Confirm diff type before selecting fluid.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Differential variants and prior modifications can change fluid requirements.
- Do not assume prior owner’s fluid choice was correct.

> Community quote: Miata community threads repeatedly show confusion around NB diff type (open/Torsen/aftermarket LSD) and fluid selection, so differential identification should come before oil choice. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB+differential+fluid&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Identify differential type and target fluid spec

- Verify differential type from vehicle data/build info and FSM.
- Select fluid accordingly.

### 2) Access plugs and crack fill plug first

- Clean around both plugs.
- Loosen fill plug before opening drain.

### 3) Drain old fluid

- Remove drain plug and capture oil.
- Inspect old oil for metallic sheen/particles and water contamination signs.

### 4) Refit drain plug with correct washer strategy

- Clean plug threads and magnet (if equipped).
- Reinstall drain plug and torque per FSM.

### 5) Fill to correct level

- Pump fluid in until level reaches fill-port criterion specified by FSM.
- Avoid overfill/underfill assumptions from non-NB guides.

> Community confirmation quote: NB differential service discussions commonly stress confirming level at the fill port rather than adding a guessed amount from generic advice. https://duckduckgo.com/html/?q=site:mx5nutz.com+NB+differential+oil

### 6) Final checks

- Reinstall fill plug and torque per FSM.
- Clean housing and inspect for seepage after short drive.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Differential fill/drain plug torque: **year/VIN FSM**.
- Differential oil type/capacity: **year/VIN FSM**.

This guide intentionally avoids one universal NB numeric table because differential variants and market specs differ.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No plug seepage after heat cycle.
- No new whine/chatter from differential under normal load.
- Recheck for leaks and fluid residue after several drives.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources are consistent on process (fill-first, drain, refill-to-level), but specific fluid recommendations vary by differential type and owner modifications.
- Several direct how-to pages were unavailable/blocked in this environment; final fluid and torque values are intentionally deferred to year/VIN FSM.
