## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)**.

A shifter rebuild is a precision feel/maintenance service: worn boots, bushing slop, and missing anti-rattle parts can make the gearbox feel worse than it is.

> Warning: Keep the shifter opening clean during service—debris entering the turret/transmission can cause long-term wear. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

> High-risk quote: NB owners frequently report that 5-speed and 6-speed shifter rebuild parts are not interchangeable in all cases; wrong kit selection leads to poor fit and continued slop. https://duckduckgo.com/html/?q=site:forum.miata.net+NB+6-speed+shifter+rebuild

> Manual-reference quote: NB FSM procedures are year-grouped (1999-2001 and 2005), so final part selection, assembly orientation, and torque specs must be confirmed by year/VIN documentation. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Trim tools for console/boot removal
- Basic sockets/spanners
- Picks/small pliers for clips and bushings
- Lint-free towels and solvent-safe cleaner
- Correct lubricant for turret/shifter pivot per variant

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Rebuild kit matched to gearbox type (5-speed vs 6-speed)
- Inner/outer shifter boots as required
- Anti-rattle bushings/spring/retainer parts where applicable
- Turret lubricant if servicing turret simultaneously

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Many 5-speed setups use common bushing/boot kits; still verify by VIN and transmission code.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- 6-speed shifter assembly details often differ from 5-speed rebuild assumptions.
- Confirm kit contents against your exact mechanism before disassembly.

> Community quote: Miata forum threads for NB 6-speed shifters repeatedly document mismatched expectations on bushing and boot parts when using generic rebuild kits. https://duckduckgo.com/html/?q=site:forum.miata.net+NB+6-speed+shifter+rebuild

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Baseline shift-feel diagnosis

- Note current symptoms: side-to-side slop, weak centering, rattle, notchiness.
- Confirm clutch disengagement is healthy before blaming shifter hardware.

### 2) Disassemble console and boot stack

- Remove knob, console trim, and both boots.
- Inspect for torn/delaminated boots and missing fasteners.

### 3) Open turret and inspect components

- Inspect lower bushing wear, anti-rattle components, and pivot wear marks.
- Document orientation before removal.

### 4) Replace wear components in matched set

- Install new bushings/anti-rattle parts per kit and FSM orientation.
- Replace cracked boots to restore sealing and NVH control.

### 5) Lubricate and reassemble

- Apply correct lubricant strategy for your variant (do not mix assumptions from other gearboxes).
- Reassemble shifter and boots carefully to avoid pinches.

### 6) Functional verification

- Check gate engagement in all gears engine-off and then running.
- Confirm reduced play/rattle and proper centering.

> Community confirmation quote: r/Miata NB shifter rebuild discussions commonly report that complete boot+bushing refresh gives the biggest improvement, while partial fixes leave residual play. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB%20shifter%20rebuild&restrict_sr=1

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Shifter plate fastener torque and turret lubricant quantity are year/transmission specific.
- Use year/VIN FSM values for final assembly.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Shift throw feels consistent and gates are clearly defined.
- No abnormal rattle at idle/cruise.
- No fluid seepage around shifter/turret service area.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public guidance agrees on the rebuild concept, but exact 5-speed vs 6-speed parts/assembly differences are source-dependent.
- This guide intentionally defers exact part numbers and torque values to year/VIN FSM and confirmed kit documentation.
