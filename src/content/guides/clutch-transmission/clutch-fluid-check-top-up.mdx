## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on a level surface with engine cool.
- Use only fresh fluid from a sealed container of the correct type.
- Avoid spilling hydraulic/brake fluid on paint.

> Warning: “brake fluid can cause serious harm if you get it in your eyes… Brake fluid will also make your paint peel off…” https://www.carcarekiosk.com/video/2001_Mazda_Miata_LS_1.8L_4_Cyl./brake_fluid/add_fluid

## Required Tools

- Clean lint-free cloths
- Small flashlight
- Nitrile gloves and eye protection
- Small clean funnel (optional)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Fresh DOT brake fluid that matches your reservoir cap/manual marking (commonly DOT 3 or DOT 4 on NB)
- Small top-up quantity is usually enough for level correction (**~100-250 ml**); buy **500 ml to 1 L** if you may bleed/flush

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 and NB2 both use a hydraulic clutch with a dedicated reservoir near the firewall area.
- Reservoir cap text and level markings can vary by market/year.

> Manual-reference quote: “The Manuals are in PDF format. You can use Adobe Reader to read or print them.” https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Inspect reservoir level and color

The clutch reservoir is the small translucent plastic tank at the rear of the engine bay near the firewall (driver side on LHD, passenger side on RHD), near the brake master area.

Check that fluid sits between MIN and MAX marks. If dark/dirty, plan a full flush/bleed rather than repeated top-ups.

### 2) Clean cap area first

Wipe dust around the cap before opening so contamination does not enter the hydraulic system.

### 3) Top up slowly to the correct mark

Add only enough fluid to reach the proper level. Do not overfill.

> High-risk quote: “When adding fluid… check your owners manual for the correct type… likely either DOT 3, DOT 4 or DOT 5… Do not shake the fluid before pouring it…” https://www.carcarekiosk.com/video/2001_Mazda_Miata_LS_1.8L_4_Cyl./brake_fluid/add_fluid

### 4) Refit cap and wipe spills immediately

Ensure cap seals properly and clean any spilled fluid from painted surfaces at once.

### 5) Pedal feel check

Press the clutch pedal several times. It should feel consistent and return normally.

### 6) If fluid dropped noticeably, inspect for hydraulic leaks

On NB cars, recurring low clutch fluid is commonly linked to master/slave cylinder issues or trapped air after previous work.

> Community quote: “I refill the clutch reservoir… then park the car for a week… the reservoir becomes mysteriously bone dry…” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=728667

> Community confirmation quote: “Fluid disappearing is most likely a bad slave cylinder… like 98.9%.” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?p=10948928

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- No torque value is normally required for a basic level check/top-up.
- Use exact year/VIN manual specs if any hydraulic component is removed.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Fluid level remains stable over several drives
- Pedal engagement point is consistent
- No dampness at master cylinder, line joints, or slave cylinder boot

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Publicly accessible sources in this environment do not provide one reliably extracted NB-only clutch reservoir capacity figure.
- Fluid specification labeling can vary by region/model year; always follow your owner/manual cap marking first.
