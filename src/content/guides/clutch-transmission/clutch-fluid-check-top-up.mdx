## Before You Start / Safety

- Engine off, car level, and paintwork protected with a rag around reservoir area.
- Use only fresh fluid from a sealed bottle.
- Clutch/brake fluid damages paint quickly—wipe spills immediately.

## Required Tools

- Nitrile gloves + eye protection
- Flashlight
- Clean lint-free cloth
- Small clean funnel/syringe (optional, helps avoid overfill)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- DOT fluid matching the reservoir-cap marking (commonly DOT 3 or DOT 4 on NB)
- Top-up volume is usually small (**~50-200 ml**)
- Buy **500 ml to 1 L** if you may also bleed clutch circuit

### Practical fluid handling notes

- If your bottle has been open for months, replace it; brake/clutch fluid absorbs moisture from air.
- If fluid appears very dark/brown, schedule a full clutch hydraulic bleed instead of repeated top-ups.

## Where the clutch reservoir is (NB)

On most NB cars, the clutch reservoir is a **small translucent plastic tank with a black screw cap** at the rear of engine bay near the firewall, close to the brake master-cylinder area.

- On LHD cars: typically driver-side rear of bay.
- On RHD cars: mirror location on passenger-side rear of bay.

Quick identification tip:
- Brake-fluid reservoir is larger and mounted directly on the brake master cylinder.
- Clutch reservoir is smaller and usually offset nearby with its own feed line.

Look for MIN/MAX marks molded into the side of the reservoir body.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Check level and condition before opening

With car level, inspect reservoir fluid height against MIN/MAX marks.

- Slightly below MAX can be normal.
- At/below MIN requires top-up and leak check.
- Very dark fluid means plan a bleed/flush soon (not repeated topping-up only).

### 2) Clean around cap first

Wipe cap and surrounding area so dirt cannot drop into the hydraulic circuit.

### 3) Open cap and top up carefully

Add fluid slowly to just below MAX using a small funnel/syringe for control.

Do **not** overfill (fluid expands with heat and can overflow).

If fluid level was at or below MIN, treat this as a leak-warning state and perform leak checks before returning to regular driving.

### 4) Refit cap and perform pedal check

Close cap, then press clutch pedal 10-15 times:
- pedal should return consistently,
- engagement point should feel stable.

### 5) Leak triage if fluid keeps dropping

Repeated fluid loss usually indicates a leak, not “normal consumption.”

Inspect in this order:
1. Clutch master cylinder area at firewall (inside and engine-bay side)
2. Hard/soft hydraulic line joints
3. Slave cylinder body and rubber boot area on transmission side

If reservoir keeps dropping, repair leak and bleed system rather than repeated top-ups.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Level remains stable over several days
- No wetness at master/slave/line unions
- Pedal return and engagement consistent hot and cold

## Quick troubleshooting cues

- Pedal slowly sinking at stops: possible internal master-cylinder bypass.
- Engagement point suddenly near floor: possible hydraulic leak or air ingress.
- Reservoir repeatedly drops after top-up: repair leak and bleed system; do not continue indefinite topping-up.
