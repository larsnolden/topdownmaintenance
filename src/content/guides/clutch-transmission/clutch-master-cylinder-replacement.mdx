## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)**.

Replace the clutch master cylinder only after confirming symptoms are hydraulic (not clutch mechanical failure alone).

> Warning: Brake/clutch fluid damages paint; protect painted surfaces and clean spills immediately. https://www.autozone.com/diy

> High-risk quote: Air introduced during clutch hydraulic service can cause loss of disengagement and persistent soft pedal if bleeding sequence is not controlled. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/clutch-master-slave-replace-or-repair.126832/

> Manual-reference quote: NB procedures/specs are year-grouped (1999-2001 and 2005), so pushrod setup, fasteners, and fluid specifications must be checked by year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Flare/line wrench for hydraulic fitting
- Basic sockets/spanners
- Bleed hose and catch bottle
- Fresh DOT fluid per FSM
- Helper or pressure/vacuum bleeder

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct clutch master cylinder for exact NB variant
- New sealing washers/gaskets where required
- Fresh clutch hydraulic fluid

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm failure point first

- Check for leaks at master, line, and slave.
- Confirm pedal sinks/does not build pressure after bleeding attempts.

### 2) Prepare and isolate system

- Protect paint around reservoir.
- Remove old fluid as practical.
- Disconnect hydraulic line carefully.

### 3) Remove master cylinder

- Disconnect pedal linkage clevis/pin.
- Remove firewall mounting hardware.
- Withdraw master cylinder.

### 4) Prime/bench-bleed replacement (if applicable)

- Pre-fill/prime master to reduce trapped air before full system bleed.
- Keep ports and seals clean.

### 5) Install and reconnect

- Install master on firewall and reconnect pedal linkage.
- Reconnect hydraulic line without cross-threading.
- Set pedal free-play/pushrod only per FSM guidance.

> Community quote: MX-5 owners report that replacing master/slave parts without careful bleeding and linkage checks can leave poor disengagement unchanged. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/clutch-master-slave-replace-or-repair.126832/

### 6) Bleed clutch hydraulic system fully

- Follow consistent pedal/bleeder timing.
- Keep reservoir above minimum at all times.
- Continue until clear fluid/no bubbles and stable pedal.

> Community confirmation quote: r/Miata NB threads repeatedly show residual air as the most common reason for post-repair poor clutch feel. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=NB+clutch+master+cylinder&restrict_sr=on

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Master cylinder mounting and line fitting torques: **use year/VIN FSM**.
- Fluid type/spec: **use year/VIN FSM**.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Pedal returns consistently with firm feel.
- Reverse/1st engage cleanly without excessive grind.
- No leaks at master, fitting, or slave after test drive.
