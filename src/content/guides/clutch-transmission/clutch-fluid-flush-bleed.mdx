## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

Clutch hydraulic flushing is different from a simple top-up: the goal is to push old/dark fluid and trapped air out of the slave bleeder while preventing reservoir run-dry.

> Warning: Brake/clutch fluid damages paint and skin; protect bodywork, wear gloves/eye protection, and clean spills immediately. https://www.autozone.com/diy

> High-risk quote: Introducing air during clutch bleeding (or letting reservoir run low) can worsen disengagement and pedal feel rather than fixing it. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/how-do-i-bleed-clutch-after-slave-cylinder-swap.86271/

> Manual-reference quote: NB manuals are split by year groups (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact fluid type spec, bleeder location details, and final checks must be confirmed by year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Fresh DOT-spec fluid per FSM
- Clear bleed hose and catch bottle
- Correct wrench for slave bleeder nipple
- Helper (for pedal method) or one-person pressure/vacuum bleeder
- Rags and paint-safe protection around reservoir

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Fresh clutch hydraulic fluid (FSM specification)
- Bleeder cap replacement if missing/damaged
- Slave/master seals only if leak evidence is found

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Typical process is unchanged: reservoir management + slave bleed + pedal verification.
- Access to slave bleeder can be tight depending on underbody hardware.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Similar bleeding process; routing/access can differ with market trims and aftermarket braces/exhaust.
- Confirm bleeder orientation and hose routing before opening nipple.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Baseline symptom check

- Confirm pedal feel issue (spongy, low bite point, incomplete disengagement).
- Check for visible leaks at master, hard line, flexible line, and slave cylinder.

### 2) Prepare reservoir and bleeder

- Clean cap area before opening reservoir.
- Fill to proper level with fresh fluid.
- Fit clear hose to slave bleeder and route to catch bottle.

### 3) Perform bleed cycles (pedal method)

- Helper depresses clutch pedal and holds.
- Open bleeder briefly, allow fluid/air to exit, close bleeder.
- Release pedal only after bleeder is closed.
- Repeat until clean fluid and no bubbles appear.

> Community quote: MX5Nutz clutch-bleed discussion highlights common mistakes around bleed sequence timing (pedal/bleeder coordination) and reservoir level control. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/how-do-i-bleed-clutch-after-slave-cylinder-swap.86271/

### 4) Keep reservoir topped up continuously

- Do not let fluid level drop below minimum at any point.
- Refill frequently during bleed sequence.

### 5) Final bleed and pedal check

- Tighten bleeder to spec (FSM).
- Confirm pedal firmness and consistent return.
- Confirm clutch disengages cleanly into reverse/first without grind.

### 6) Re-test after short drive

- Recheck reservoir level.
- Verify no weeping at bleeder or line fittings.

> Community confirmation quote: r/Miata NB clutch-bleed discussions repeatedly show that unresolved pedal issues after bleeding often trace to persistent air, worn slave/master seals, or line issues—not just fluid age. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=NB+clutch+bleed&restrict_sr=on

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Pedal feel is firm and repeatable.
- Gear engagement improves (especially reverse/1st from stop).
- No leaks at slave bleeder, line unions, or master/slave seals.
