## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Differential mount/bushing work is safety-sensitive because the differential is heavy and tied to driveline geometry.

> Warning: "Remember safety first, never get under the vehicle unless jack stands are in place." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/rear-axle-seal-replacement

> High-risk quote: "Replacing a motor mount always requires supporting the engine and/or transmission. The weight must be taken off the mount before it can be unbolted and replaced." https://www.aa1car.com/library/motor_mounts.htm

Apply the same support principle to the NB differential assembly: support first, then unbolt.

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Transmission/differential support jack (or stable secondary support)
- Socket set, breaker bar, torque wrench
- Pry bar for bushing movement checks
- Paint marker for baseline alignment marks
- Light and inspection mirror

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Differential mount/bushing kit matched to NB differential/PPF setup
- New fasteners/nuts where Mazda or kit instructions specify one-time use
- Optional: replacement hardware sleeves/washers depending on kit

- Verify what is actually installed before ordering: many cars now have mixed NA/NB or aftermarket drivetrain parts.

### NB2 (2001-2005)

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm mount/bushing symptoms before teardown

Typical clues are driveline thump/clunk on throttle transitions, excess differential movement under load changes, and increased NVH.

> High-risk quote: "When a motor or transmission mount fails... [it] can produce thumping and rattling noises" and should be inspected during driveline service. https://www.aa1car.com/library/motor_mounts.htm

### 2) Rule out look-alike faults first

Before bushing replacement, inspect:
- differential leaks and pinion/output play,
- driveshaft fastener condition,
- transmission mount condition,
- exhaust contact points.

Do not assume every rear driveline clunk is a diff bushing.

### 3) Baseline and support

- Mark mount positions where practical.
- Support the differential/PPF load before loosening mount fasteners.
- Loosen hardware in controlled sequence (per FSM).

### 4) Remove and inspect old bushings/mounts

Look for:
- torn or separated rubber,
- collapsed bushing height,
- ovalized sleeves,
- signs of metal-to-metal contact.

If sleeves/bores are damaged, do not force new bushings into compromised housings.

### 5) Install new bushings/mounts

- Use correct orientation per kit/FSM.
- Keep sleeves centered and seated.
- Snug all hardware first, then final-torque in FSM sequence.

> Warning: Generic online procedures are not enough for final torque: always verify year/VIN-specific NB mounting sequence and torque values in workshop data. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

### 6) Initial validation

- Recheck fasteners after settling the assembly.
- Verify clearance to PPF/exhaust/subframe areas.
- Road-test for clunk, lash feel, and new vibration.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Differential mount and related bracket torque values: **year/VIN FSM only**
- PPF-related fastener sequencing/alignment values: **year/VIN FSM only**

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Clunk/thump reduced or eliminated on throttle on/off transitions
- No fresh driveline vibration introduced at cruise
- No new contact marks or hardware movement after recheck
