## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

Clutch replacement is major work requiring gearbox removal. Plan lift/support tools and don’t rush alignment and torque steps.

> Warning: Transmission removal requires secure vehicle support and stable gearbox handling; uncontrolled movement can cause serious injury. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-a-clutch

> High-risk quote: Clutch dust and debris can be hazardous; avoid compressed-air dust blowout and use controlled cleaning methods. https://www.wikihow.com/Change-a-Clutch

> Manual-reference quote: NB clutch/transmission procedures and torque values are year-grouped in FSM sets (1999-2001 and 2005), so final specs must be matched to exact year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Jack/stands or lift
- Transmission jack/support
- Socket/extension/breaker bar set
- Torque wrench(es)
- Clutch alignment tool (usually in clutch kit)
- Thread prep/cleaner and marking pen

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Clutch disc
- Pressure plate
- Release (throwout) bearing
- Pilot bearing/bushing (where equipped)
- Gearbox oil if drain/refill performed
- Optional while-open: rear main seal, input shaft seal, clutch fork pivot hardware (condition-dependent)

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Commonly simpler drivetrain packaging but still verify gearbox variant and clutch dimensions before ordering.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- 5-speed vs 6-speed and market differences can affect clutch kit and flywheel compatibility.
- Confirm kit contents against actual gearbox/flywheel setup before teardown.

> Community quote: NB owners repeatedly report parts-ordering confusion across NB variants, so gearbox/clutch identification before purchase is critical. https://duckduckgo.com/html/?q=site:mx5nutz.com+NB+clutch+replacement

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm clutch diagnosis before replacement

- Check for slip under load, release issues, chatter, and bite-point shift.
- Rule out hydraulic faults first (master/slave/air).

### 2) Prepare drivetrain removal

- Disconnect battery.
- Remove required underbody components/propshaft interfaces per FSM.
- Support gearbox and engine appropriately.

### 3) Remove gearbox and inspect clutch assembly

- Remove transmission from bellhousing.
- Inspect pressure plate/disc wear pattern and release mechanism condition.
- Inspect flywheel surface for heat spots/scoring/cracks.

### 4) Replace clutch wear components as a matched set

- Remove old pressure plate/disc.
- Replace release bearing and pilot bearing where applicable.
- Use alignment tool for disc centering.

> High-risk quote: Uneven pressure-plate tightening or poor disc alignment can cause immediate engagement/drivability problems after reassembly. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-a-clutch

### 5) While-you’re-there checks

- Evaluate rear main seal area for leakage.
- Inspect input shaft seal and fork/pivot contact wear.
- Replace only confirmed-worn components to avoid repeat labor.

### 6) Reassemble and torque in proper sequence

- Torque clutch/flywheel-related fasteners per exact FSM values and sequence.
- Reinstall gearbox and driveline components.
- Refill fluids and reconnect systems.

### 7) Final hydraulic and functional checks

- Bleed clutch hydraulics if disturbed.
- Verify pedal feel and clean disengagement into reverse/1st.
- Road-test for slip/chatter/noise.

> Community confirmation quote: r/Miata NB clutch replacement discussions consistently highlight that post-job issues are often alignment/bleed/installation-detail related rather than defective new clutch parts. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB%20clutch%20replacement&restrict_sr=1

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Smooth take-up, no abnormal slip, no new chatter.
- Reverse engagement without severe grind after proper bleed.
- No leaks from disturbed seals/joints after heat cycles.
