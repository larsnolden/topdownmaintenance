## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Driveshaft work is safety-critical: if a U-joint fails fully, the shaft can separate and cause loss of control or major underbody damage.

> Warning: "Remember safety first, never get under the vehicle unless jack stands are in place." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-u-joints-work

> High-risk quote: "if it fails completely, it can separate in catastrophic fashion and cause you to lose control while you drive." https://www.autozone.com/diy/drivetrain/how-to-replace-u-joints

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Wheel chocks
- Socket set and torque wrench
- Paint marker (for flange orientation marks)
- Pry bar for play checks
- Safety glasses and gloves

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Replacement driveshaft assembly or serviceable U-joints (only if your shaft design supports service)
- New lock hardware/retainers where specified
- Grease only if serviceable greaseable joints are installed

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Verify exact driveshaft type and flange/hardware before ordering parts.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same verification required; many cars now have mixed driveline parts from previous repairs/swaps.

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens lists separate NB-era factory manuals (including 1999-2001 and 2005 sets), so driveshaft procedures and tightening values must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before final assembly. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptom pattern first

Typical worn-joint/shaft clues are rotational squeak/tick, clunk on load transitions, or speed-related vibration.

> High-risk quote: "When a U joint fails it can cause rhythmic squeaking or ticking... and if left unattended the universal joint can break". https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-u-joints-work

### 2) Inspect on lift/stands before removal

- Check for visible rust dust near bearing caps.
- Check each joint for stiffness, notchiness, or free play.
- Check flange hardware and surrounding mount condition.

### 3) Mark orientation before disassembly

Mark shaft-to-flange relationship to preserve balance orientation if reusing the shaft.

> High-risk quote: "Before disassembly, mark the relationship between the drive shaft and the flanges to ensure proper alignment upon reinstallation." https://www.autozone.com/diy/drivetrain/how-to-replace-u-joints

### 4) Determine service path: joint-only vs complete shaft

Many NB owners encounter non-serviceable OEM-style joints where practical repair is full shaft replacement.

> Community quote: "The resonant noise made me think u-joints but the Miata doesn’t have serviceable u-joints." https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=nb+driveshaft+u+joint&restrict_sr=on

If serviceability is uncertain, verify by part catalog + FSM + physical inspection before committing to press-out work.

### 5) Install and reassemble carefully

- Keep mating surfaces clean.
- Refit in marked orientation where applicable.
- Final-torque all fasteners to year/VIN FSM values.

### 6) Post-install validation

- Road-test for takeoff clunk and speed-related vibration.
- Recheck fasteners after initial heat cycle.
- If vibration persists, inspect mounts, differential lash, wheel bearings, and wheel balance before condemning new shaft parts.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Driveshaft flange fasteners: **year/VIN FSM only**
- Related bracket/PPF hardware: **year/VIN FSM only**

No universal publish-safe NB torque table is included here because hardware and prior modifications vary.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No clunk when taking up load in low gears
- No new speed-dependent vibration
- No loosened flange hardware at recheck
