## Before You Start / Safety

- Park on level ground, handbrake on, engine OFF for adjustment steps.
- Do not force pedal-stop or pushrod locknuts; access is tight and threads can be damaged.
- Confirm hydraulic condition first (fluid level, leaks) before changing adjustment.

> Warning: “Adjusting a clutch pedal can also prevent slipping… testing a clutch pedal to see if you want to make adjustments is easy…” https://www.doityourself.com/stry/how-to-adjust-a-clutch-pedal

## Required Tools

- Small spanners/wrenches for locknuts
- Flashlight/headlamp
- Ruler or caliper (for pedal free-play check)
- Trim/panel tools if lower dash trim needs removal

## Required Parts / Fluids

- None for adjustment itself
- Optional: clutch fluid if hydraulic level is low

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1/NB2 pedal-box geometry is similar, but previous owner adjustments can leave non-standard settings.
- Adjust in small increments, then verify engagement point and full release.

> Manual-reference quote: “The Manuals are in PDF format. You can use Adobe Reader to read or print them.” https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Baseline pedal behavior

Record current free play and engagement point before touching locknuts.

Practical starting target used by many NB owners is about **3/8 in (~9-10 mm)** of top-of-pedal free play, then fine-tune for clean engagement.

### 2) Confirm hydraulic health first

If fluid is missing or pedal is inconsistent, repair leaks/air first; adjustment alone will not fix a failing master/slave setup.

### 3) Access the pedal adjuster area

Work under the driver-side dash at the clutch pedal pushrod/locknut area.

### 4) Adjust free play in small steps

Loosen locknut, rotate adjuster slightly (about 1/4 turn at a time), re-lock, and re-test pedal feel and shift engagement each time.

- Too little free play: clutch may not fully clamp (slip risk).
- Too much free play: clutch may not fully release (drag/gear crunch risk).

> Community quote: “The manual says something like 3/8" of free play.” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=159669

### 5) Check pedal height relationship

Ensure clutch pedal sits in a sensible relationship to brake pedal and still has some free movement at the top.

Practical check: if clutch pedal sits unusually high/low versus brake pedal after adjustment, re-baseline and re-adjust in smaller increments.

> Community confirmation quote: “There’s also a spec on the clutch pedal height… close to the height of the brake pedal…” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=159669

### 6) Verify no drag and no slip

Road test after warm-up: confirm easy gear selection at standstill and no flare/slip under moderate acceleration.

> Community confirmation quote: “A slave replacement six months later had no more freeplay in pedal.” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=342162

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- No published universal torque value is provided here for pedal adjuster locknuts (varies by hardware/access).
- Tighten locknuts securely without over-torquing in confined pedal-box area.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Smooth 1st/reverse engagement at stop without crunch
- No clutch slip under load
- Engagement point remains stable over several drives
- No new pedal squeak/bind after adjustment

- This run could not reliably extract one NB-only official numeric free-play + pedal-height table row from machine-readable factory documents.
- Community references consistently mention free-play targeting and pedal-height relationship, but exact preferred value can vary by driver and clutch wear state.
