## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

PPF work is high-risk because it couples transmission and differential alignment. Incorrect adjustment can create vibration, shift feel problems, or hardware stress.

> Warning: "Remember safety first, never get under the vehicle unless jack stands are in place." https://www.autozone.com/diy/drivetrain/how-to-replace-u-joints

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens provides separate NB-era FSM sets (1999-2001 and 2005), so PPF procedures and any alignment/torque values must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before adjustment. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Support jack for transmission/differential load control
- Marker/paint pen for baseline position marks
- Torque wrench (final values from year/VIN FSM)
- Straightedge/measurement tools required by your FSM version

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Usually none for inspection-only work
- Replacement bolts/nuts if damaged or specified as one-time-use
- Any mounts/bushings found defective during inspection

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Confirm all driveline parts are truly NB-spec before alignment; many cars have mixed components.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: swaps and prior repairs are common; inspect actual installed hardware first.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm this is a PPF-related symptom

Typical triggers after driveline work include new vibration, clunk on load transitions, or altered shift feel.

> High-risk quote: "If you experience driveline vibrations after replacement, it’s possible that the drive shaft is out of alignment." https://www.autozone.com/diy/drivetrain/how-to-replace-u-joints

Do not adjust PPF first if symptoms clearly point to wheel/tire or unrelated engine issues.

### 2) Inspect fundamentals before touching alignment

- Verify transmission mount and differential mount condition.
- Check for loose/damaged driveline fasteners.
- Confirm driveshaft orientation marks were preserved during reassembly.

> High-risk quote: "Before disassembly, mark the relationship between the drive shaft and the flanges to ensure proper alignment upon reinstallation." https://www.autozone.com/diy/drivetrain/how-to-replace-u-joints

### 3) Baseline and controlled loosen sequence

- Mark current PPF and related fastener positions.
- Support drivetrain so mount preload is controlled.
- Loosen only in FSM-prescribed order and extent.

### 4) Basic realignment (FSM-guided only)

- Follow FSM reference points and sequence for your year/VIN.
- Center/settle assembly as specified.
- Re-torque in the exact FSM order.

> Warning: This guide intentionally does **not** publish universal numeric PPF alignment or torque values; use your exact NB FSM to avoid misalignment damage. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

### 5) Recheck and road test

- Verify no hardware movement after initial test.
- Confirm shift feel and driveline NVH under gentle and moderate load.
- Re-inspect for witness marks/contact after heat cycle.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- PPF and related mount fasteners: **year/VIN FSM only**
- Driveline flange hardware values: **year/VIN FSM only**

No universal publish-safe NB-wide PPF torque/alignment value table is provided here.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No new or worsened vibration across prior complaint speeds
- No clunk on throttle on/off transitions
- No fastener shift relative to your baseline marks
