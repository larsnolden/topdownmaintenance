## Before You Start / Safety

- Car on level ground, securely on stands, wheels removed.
- Brake fluid strips paint quickly—cover fender areas and wipe spills immediately.
- Never allow master-cylinder reservoir to run dry.

## Required Tools

- Jack, axle stands, wheel chocks
- Clear bleed hose + waste bottle
- Flare/bleeder wrench (commonly 8 mm or 10 mm depending caliper)
- Gloves, eye protection, rags
- Helper (for 2-person method) or pressure bleeder

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Fresh unopened brake fluid matching reservoir-cap/manual spec
- NB flush planning quantity: **1.0 L minimum** (buy more if fluid is very dark or you re-bleed)

### Practical fluid guidance

- DOT 4 glycol-based fluid is a common NB service choice.
- Example DOT 4 products widely used: ATE SL, ATE SL.6, ATE Typ 200.
- Do **not** use DOT 5 silicone fluid in DOT 3/4 designed systems.

## NB orientation details

- Reservoir is mounted on brake master cylinder at rear of engine bay near firewall.
- Each caliper bleeder nipple is a small hex nipple near the top of caliper body, usually protected by a rubber cap.
- Keep nipple top area clean before opening.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Pre-fill and baseline

Open reservoir and fill near MAX with fresh fluid.
Do not reuse old/open fluid.

### 2) Bleed sequence

Use a consistent far-to-near sequence from the master cylinder:

1. Right Rear (RR)
2. Left Rear (LR)
3. Right Front (RF)
4. Left Front (LF)

Keep the same order throughout one full flush cycle.

### 3) Bleed one corner at a time

For each caliper:
1. Fit clear hose onto bleeder nipple.
2. Crack bleeder ~1/4 turn.
3. 2-person method: helper presses pedal and holds; close bleeder before helper releases pedal.
4. Repeat until fresh clean fluid appears with no bubbles.

If using pressure bleeder, maintain stable pressure and still watch reservoir level.

### 4) Reservoir control rule (critical)

After every few pedal strokes or each corner segment, top reservoir up.

If reservoir drops too low and ingests air, you must re-bleed affected circuits.

### 5) Final corner checks

At each corner after final bleed:
- snug bleeder,
- clean nipple area,
- reinstall rubber cap.

### 6) Final reservoir set and pedal test

Set reservoir near MAX, cap securely.
With engine OFF, pump pedal until firm.
Start engine and confirm pedal remains consistent (slight assist drop is normal, sinking/spongy feel is not).

### 7) Controlled road verification

Perform low-speed stop checks first (parking-lot speed), then normal traffic braking only after pedal is consistently firm.

## Post-service verification checklist

- Pedal firm and repeatable
- No seepage at any bleeder nipple
- No warning lights related to braking system
- Straight, stable braking during test stops
