## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on a level surface with the car securely supported.
- Brake fluid damages paint; keep water/rags ready and wipe spills immediately.
- Never let the master cylinder run dry during bleeding.

> Warning: “If it’s full, some fluid needs to be removed. This will help avoid spillage due to overflow when compressing the brake caliper piston.” https://www.autozone.com/diy/brakes/how-to-replace-brake-pads-and-rotors

## Required Tools

- Jack, axle stands, wheel chocks
- Catch bottle + clear hose for bleeders
- 8 mm / 10 mm bleeder wrench (varies by corner)
- Fresh unopened brake fluid bottles
- Gloves, eye protection, rags

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Brake fluid meeting your NB owner/manual specification (commonly DOT 3/4 class depending on market/year)

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 and NB2 bleed workflow is similar, but ABS/non-ABS details and line routing can change exact sequence preference.
- Treat forum sequence advice as practical guidance; confirm final sequence against your year/VIN service documentation.

> Manual-reference quote: “The Manuals are in PDF format. You can use Adobe Reader to read or print them.” https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Prepare reservoir and corners

Open reservoir, set cap loosely to reduce splashing contamination, and prefill with fresh fluid.

### 2) Start at a consistent corner order

Common NA/NB community practice is to start with the farthest corner from the master cylinder and move toward the closest.

> Community quote: “RR,LR,RF, and LF.” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=415917

### 3) Bleed each caliper while monitoring reservoir level

Open bleeder slightly, move fluid/air through clear hose into bottle, then close bleeder before pedal release (two-person method) or use pressure-bleed workflow.

> Community confirmation quote: “The easiest and fastest way is to power/pressure bleed… and will never go back to any other method.” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=612091

### 4) Keep topping up reservoir

Top up frequently; if reservoir runs low, you can pull air and must restart the process.

### 5) Continue until clean fluid/no bubbles at each corner

Move through your chosen sequence and verify clean new fluid appearance.

### 6) Finalize and road-check

Set fluid to MAX, tighten cap, reinstall wheels, then perform low-speed pedal and stopping checks before normal driving.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Wheel-nut torque and bleeder-tightening values are year/setup dependent; use your NB year/VIN manual specs.
- No single universal flush volume is guaranteed; required volume depends on how contaminated old fluid is and whether full-corner flushing is performed.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Pedal is firm and consistent engine ON/OFF
- No leaks or seepage at bleeder nipples
- Stable braking response at low speed before regular use

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Community sources disagree on whether exact sequence is critical in all ABS layouts; confidence is high on consistency + reservoir control, lower on one universal NB sequence statement.
- This run could not extract one machine-readable NB factory table with definitive DOT wording for every market/year, so final fluid spec remains owner-manual dependent.
