## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Sticking slide pins can cause uneven pad wear, brake drag, and overheating.

> Warning: "Safely raise the car in the air and secure it on jack stands while removing the wheels." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-rear-brake-pads-and-rotors

> High-risk quote: "Uneven wear is an indicator of the pad or pads ‘sticking’ due to lack of lubricant, worn pad or caliper hardware, or a sticking caliper piston." https://www.autozone.com/diy/brakes/how-to-replace-brake-pads-and-rotors

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens lists separate NB-era FSM sets (1999-2001 and 2005), so caliper hardware procedures and torque values must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before final tightening. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Wheel chocks
- Basic socket/hex tools for caliper pin fasteners
- Brake cleaner and lint-free wipes
- Nylon/brass brush
- Correct caliper slide-pin grease (brake-safe, rubber-compatible)
- Torque wrench

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Slide pin boot kit if torn/hardened
- Replacement pins if pitted or scored
- Fresh pad hardware as needed

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Verify caliper and bracket type before parts ordering; many cars now run mixed hardware.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: trim/package and prior repairs can change pin/boot details.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptom before teardown

Typical signs: one wheel hotter than the other, pad taper/uneven wear, dragging feel, or clunk from loose/sticking pin hardware.

### 2) Lift and inspect safely

- Raise and support vehicle securely.
- Remove wheel.
- Check for heat discoloration, damaged boots, and pin movement resistance.

### 3) Remove caliper and access pins

Support caliper; do not let it hang by the hose.

> High-risk quote: "Once loose, support the caliper using zip ties or bailing wire, (Do not allow the caliper to hang by the brake hose)." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-rear-brake-pads-and-rotors

### 4) Clean and inspect pins/bores

- Remove old grease completely.
- Clean pin bores and pin surfaces.
- Replace pins or boots if rusted, pitted, swollen, or torn.

> High-risk quote: "Inspect the caliper slides on the bracket and service the slides by cleaning and adding a small amount of brake grease to lubricate." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-rear-brake-pads-and-rotors

### 5) Lubricate correctly and reassemble

- Apply a thin, even coat of brake-safe slide-pin grease.
- Avoid overpacking boots.
- Confirm smooth full travel before reinstalling caliper.

> High-risk quote: "Make sure to apply brake lubricant at the slider contact points before installing the hardware." https://www.autozone.com/diy/brakes/how-to-replace-brake-pads-and-rotors

### 6) Final checks

- Torque fasteners to FSM values.
- Pump pedal to seat components.
- Road-test and compare left/right wheel temperatures afterward.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Caliper pin bolts and bracket fasteners: **year/VIN FSM only**

No universal publish-safe NB torque table is provided here because caliper package and hardware can vary.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No sticking sensation after repeated stops
- Wheel temperatures left/right are more consistent
- Pad wear trend normal on next inspection
- No caliper clunk from loose pin hardware
