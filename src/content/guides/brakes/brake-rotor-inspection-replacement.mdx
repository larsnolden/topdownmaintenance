## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on flat ground, chock wheels, and support the car securely on stands.
- Wear eye protection and gloves.
- If you are retracting caliper pistons during the job, monitor fluid level in the reservoir.

> Warning: "Park the vehicle on a dry, flat surface and install wheel chocks... Use secure jack points for the jack and jack stands." https://www.autozone.com/diy/brakes/how-to-replace-brake-pads-and-rotors

## Required Tools

- Jack, axle stands, wheel chocks
- Socket set and breaker bar
- Torque wrench
- Caliper hanger hook/wire
- Wire brush and brake cleaner
- Soft mallet and rust penetrant (for stuck rotors)
- Dial indicator (optional, for runout checks)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct front/rear rotors for your NB brake package
- Brake pads/hardware if replacing as a set (recommended when rotors are renewed)
- Brake cleaner
- Small amount of anti-seize for rotor-to-hub pilot area (only where manufacturer guidance allows; keep away from friction surfaces)

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB brake fitment varies by engine/trim/brake package; do not assume one rotor/pad shape fits every NB.
- Community reports repeatedly flag package-dependent differences (including sport vs non-sport setups), so verify by VIN/removed-part dimensions before installation.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "Pads are NOT the same. Different between 1.6, 1.8 and 1.8 Sport." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=664233

> Community confirmation quote: "My understanding is that all 03-05 came with ss, and therfore big brakes." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=426617

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Inspect before disassembly

Check for:
- deep grooves/scoring,
- cracking/heat checking,
- lip at outer edge,
- thickness below service limit,
- pedal pulsation or steering-wheel shake under braking.

If symptoms are present at one axle, inspect both sides and replace rotors in axle pairs.

### 2) Remove wheel, caliper, and bracket

- Loosen wheel nuts with the car on the ground, then lift/support.
- Remove caliper and support it so the hose is not loaded.
- Remove caliper bracket if required for rotor removal.

> Warning: "Do not allow the caliper to hang by the brake hose" https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-front-brake-pads-and-rotors-fwd

### 3) Remove rotor

- If rotor is stuck by corrosion, apply penetrant at hub/rotor interface and tap with a soft mallet.
- Some cars require retaining screws/alternative extraction methods; follow FSM for your exact NB year.

### 4) Clean the hub face thoroughly

This is critical: rust/debris between hub and rotor can cause lateral runout and pedal pulsation even with new parts.

> High-risk quote: "The hub surface needs to be cleaned with a wire brush to remove rust and debris. This will ensure the new rotor sits flush on the hub and eliminates the possibility of pedal pulsation." https://www.autozone.com/diy/brakes/how-to-replace-brake-pads-and-rotors

### 5) Install new rotor and reassemble

- Degrease new rotor friction surfaces with brake cleaner.
- Install rotor squarely on the hub.
- Refit bracket/caliper and torque fasteners to year/VIN-specific Mazda data.
- If installing new pads too, service slider hardware as required and use only approved lubricant points.

### 6) Pedal reset and bedding

- Before moving the car, pump pedal until firm.
- Bed new rotor/pad surfaces with controlled moderate stops and cooldown.

> High-risk quote: "The break-in procedure is critical to brake performance." https://www.powerstop.com/resources/brake-pad-break-in-procedure/

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Rotor, caliper bracket, caliper slide, and wheel-lug torque values are year/package-specific on NB.
- Use your exact NB1/NB2 factory service information before final tightening.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Firm brake pedal with engine running.
- No fluid leaks, no hose twist, no hardware contact noise.
- Initial road test at low speed, then gradual bedding.
- Recheck wheel-lug torque after first heat cycle.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- In this environment, publicly extractable sources did not provide one reliable universal NB rotor thickness/minimum-spec table and torque matrix across all brake packages.
- Community sources help identify fitment pitfalls but are not a substitute for VIN-specific FSM values.
