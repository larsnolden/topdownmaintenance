## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Parking brake adjustment should not be used to hide worn rear brake parts. Fix pad/caliper/cable faults first, then adjust.

> Warning: "Begin with the vehicle on level ground, in park and the engine off with the parking brake released (important)." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-rear-brake-pads-and-rotors

> High-risk quote: "Safely raise the car in the air and secure it on jack stands while removing the wheels." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-rear-brake-pads-and-rotors

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens hosts NB-era factory manuals (including 1999-2001 and 2005), so final adjustment specs and torque values must be confirmed by exact year/VIN before locking settings. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Wheel chocks
- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Phillips screwdriver + 10 mm socket set for center-console removal/access
- Small deep socket/spanner for handbrake cable adjuster nut
- Marker pen/tape for baseline lever-click count notes
- Rear caliper service tools as required by your rear brake design
- Torque wrench (FSM values)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Usually none for adjustment-only work
- If inspection finds faults: rear pads/hardware, caliper service parts, or cable components as needed

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Rear brake and handbrake behavior can vary with market trim and prior parts swaps.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: many cars no longer have fully original rear-brake hardware.

Use visual confirmation of installed hardware before applying any one-size-fits-all adjustment plan.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptom and baseline

Common symptoms are excessive lever travel, weak hold on incline, or rear drag after prior work.

Baseline checklist before touching the adjuster:
- Count lever clicks from fully released to firm hold.
- Note whether the car holds on a mild incline.
- With rear wheels raised and handbrake released, spin each wheel by hand and compare drag.

One-sided behavior usually indicates caliper/cable faults, not just cable slack.

### 2) Inspect rear brake condition before adjustment

- Check rear pad thickness and uneven wear.
- Inspect caliper slides and piston movement.
- Inspect cable routing and free return.

> High-risk quote: "Rear brake pads are similar to a front brake job except on a front brake job there is no parking brake assembly to deal with." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-rear-brake-pads-and-rotors

### 3) Identify rear parking brake style behavior

2CarPros describes two common rear disc parking brake arrangements (integrated caliper mechanism vs drum-in-hat style).

> High-risk quote: "One style of parking brake will use the brake caliper to apply the parking brakes... The second style ... uses a small brake shoe arrangement inside the rotor hub." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-rear-brake-pads-and-rotors

On NB cars, treat this as **inspection context only** and confirm your exact mechanism in FSM and by physical inspection.

### 4) Perform adjustment conservatively

On NB cars, cable adjustment is at the handbrake lever equalizer under the center-console area.

Typical access path:
1. Release handbrake fully.
2. Remove center-console trim around the lever.
3. Locate the threaded rod/equalizer and lock/adjusting nut at the base of the lever.

Adjustment method:
- Set rear brake mechanism condition first (no sticking hardware).
- Turn adjuster in **small increments** (about 1/2 turn at a time).
- After each change, cycle lever 2-3 times, then re-check click count and wheel free-rotation.
- Recheck both sides for equal release and engagement.
- Stop immediately if you create wheel drag at rest.

### 5) Drag vs travel validation

Target outcome is: acceptable lever travel with strong hold **without** residual drag.

Practical validation sequence:
1. Handbrake released: both rear wheels should rotate freely by hand with similar resistance.
2. Handbrake applied: both rear wheels should resist rotation similarly.
3. After release: both wheels should return to free rotation quickly (no delayed stick).

If you can only get holding force by creating drag, repair rear brake components instead of tightening further.

### 6) Finalize and road-check

- Confirm free wheel rotation with parking brake released.
- Confirm secure hold on mild incline.
- Re-check after short drive and cooldown.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Lever travel improved and repeatable
- No rear wheel drag with brake released
- Holds vehicle on incline without ratcheting excessively high
- Brake warning lamp behavior correct (illuminates with lever raised, off when released)
- No new brake heat/smell after short drive
