## Before You Start / Safety

- Never apply fresh underseal over active/flaking rust without preparation.
- Work with proper ventilation and PPE (eye, respiratory, gloves).
- Keep coatings away from brakes, exhaust hot zones, and moving joints.

> Warning: "Do not rely on the jack in the boot of your car or a trolley jack ..." https://www.autoexpress.co.uk/car-news/107732/car-underseal-what-is-it-and-is-it-worth-doing

## Required Tools

- Jack, stands, wheel chocks (or lift)
- Scraper/wire brush and rust-removal prep tools
- Degreaser and cleaning cloths
- Masking materials
- Underseal/wax application equipment (aerosol/brush/gun)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Rust converter/treatment (as required)
- Underbody protection product (wax/underseal system)
- Optional cavity wax for enclosed sections

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1/NB2 share common corrosion-prone areas around sills, arches, and lower structure.
- Drain health matters: trapped moisture can undermine fresh underseal work.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "NB rust on sill - from inside or surface rust?" https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB+rust+repair+sills&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Inspect and classify existing condition

Identify:
- intact factory coating,
- cracked/peeling old underseal,
- superficial oxidation,
- scaling/perforation requiring welding before coating.

### 2) Clean and remove failed coating locally

Remove loose/flaking underseal and rust scale before any new product is applied.

> High-risk quote: "To do the job properly you'll need to make sure the surface ... is clean and that the old, cracked underseal is removed." https://www.autoexpress.co.uk/car-news/107732/car-underseal-what-is-it-and-is-it-worth-doing

### 3) Repair rust first, then protect

If rust has progressed to soft metal or perforation, arrange proper repair before resealing.

### 4) Mask sensitive components

Mask brakes, exhaust, rubber bushings, and electrical connectors to avoid contamination.

### 5) Apply underseal in controlled layers

Apply product per manufacturer instructions and allow curing between coats where required.

> High-risk quote: "Make sure you mask off areas which shouldn't be covered, especially the brakes or the exhaust pipe." https://www.autoexpress.co.uk/car-news/107732/car-underseal-what-is-it-and-is-it-worth-doing

### 6) Recheck drains and moisture paths

After curing, verify convertible drain paths remain clear so water does not re-accumulate in sill-adjacent areas.

> Community confirmation quote: "Prevent Rust NB Miata?" https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=Prevent+Rust+NB+Miata&restrict_sr=1

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Not applicable for coating-only maintenance.
- If undertrays/fasteners are removed, use year/VIN-specific Mazda torque data.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No coating on prohibited surfaces (brakes/exhaust mating areas).
- Cured finish remains adhered after first heat/rain cycle.
- Follow-up inspection after 1-3 months for cracking or impact damage.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Publicly accessible sources agree on the sequence: inspect → remove failed coating → treat rust → apply fresh underseal.
- A single extractable NB-only factory underseal product spec and interval table was not available in this environment, so product and interval choices should be validated against year/market documentation and climate use.
