## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Puncture decisions are safety-critical. A wrong “quick fix” can lead to rapid deflation later.

> Warning: Do not rely on temporary roadside repairs for long-term highway driving. https://www.bridgestonetire.com/learn/maintenance/how-to-fix-a-flat-tire/

> High-risk quote: Repairs are generally limited to the crown/central tread area; shoulder and sidewall punctures are typically non-repairable. https://www.discounttire.com/learn/tire-repair

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so OEM tire size/load/speed requirements should be verified by year/VIN when deciding repair vs replacement. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Tire pressure gauge
- Spray bottle with soapy water (leak localization)
- Chalk/marker
- Tread depth gauge

## Required Parts / Fluids

- None for inspection-only decision
- Professional internal patch/plug combo repair (if repairable)
- Replacement tire(s) if non-repairable

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Many cars run non-stock wheels/tires; repair decision must still prioritize structural location and damage size over brand/type preference.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: mixed tire age/compound setups are common; consider pair replacement if tread mismatch becomes excessive.

When in doubt, choose inspection by a tire shop that performs internal patch/plug repairs and rejects sidewall/shoulder punctures.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm puncture and mark location

- Inflate to spec and identify leak point with soapy water.
- Mark puncture position relative to tread center and shoulder.
- Check for secondary punctures.

### 2) Screen for immediate replacement criteria

Replace tire if any of these apply:
- sidewall or shoulder puncture,
- large/irregular cut,
- prior overlapping repairs,
- run-flat/underinflation damage signs,
- exposed cords or bulge.

### 3) Assess repairability boundaries

For repair candidates, use conservative boundaries:
- puncture in central tread area only (not shoulder/sidewall),
- puncture diameter typically **≤ 6 mm (1/4 in)**,
- no casing/inner-liner separation from underinflation heat damage.

If any boundary is uncertain, treat as non-repairable and replace.

### 4) Choose repair method conservatively

- **Preferred**: internal inspection + professional patch/plug combination repair.
- **Temporary only**: external rope plug for emergency mobility.

### 5) Post-repair checks

- Recheck pressure after short drive and next day.
- Monitor for slow leak recurrence.
- Rebalance if vibration appears after repair.

### 6) Replace in pairs if needed

If replacement is required and tread depth mismatch is large, replace axle pair to keep grip/handling balanced.

Practical mismatch trigger:
- if difference across same axle is around **2/32 in (1.6 mm)** or more, pair replacement is usually the safer handling choice.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Wheel lug torque after wheel removal/reinstall: follow NB year/VIN FSM and wheel-lug guide.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Pressure stable over 24–72 hours
- No abnormal vibration at speed
- No visible tread distortion around repair area

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Repair size limits and legal acceptance vary by jurisdiction, tire type, and service standard.
- Therefore final repairability decisions are explicitly deferred to professional internal inspection and local regulations.
