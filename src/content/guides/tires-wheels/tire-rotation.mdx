## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on level ground and secure the vehicle correctly before wheel removal.
- Confirm your tires are actually rotatable in a cross/front-rear pattern (non-directional, compatible sizes).
- Re-torque wheel nuts to NB specification after wheel installation.

> Warning: "The tire rotation pattern that’s best for your vehicle will depend on ... whether your tires are directional or non-directional ..." https://tires.bridgestone.com/en-us/learn/tire-maintenance/tire-rotation

## Required Tools

- Jack and axle stands
- Wheel chocks
- Torque wrench
- Socket/breaker bar
- Chalk/marker for wheel positions

## Required Parts / Fluids

- None for rotation-only service

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1/NB2 cars may run stock square setups or mixed aftermarket wheel/tire packages.
- Rotation pattern choice must match your actual tire type, size layout, and directionality arrows.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

### Pattern selector cheat-sheet (practical NB use)

- **Square, non-directional set on RWD NB:** use **rearward cross**
- **Square, non-directional set on AWD/4WD vehicle (general reference):** use **X-pattern**
- **Directional tires:** use **front-to-back on same side only**
- **Staggered front/rear sizes:** usually **no front↔rear rotation**; at most side-to-side only if tire design permits

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm rotation eligibility first

Do **not** apply generic crossing patterns blindly. Check:
- directional arrows,
- staggered sizes,
- asymmetrical fitment restrictions,
- unusual wear that may require alignment correction before rotation.

### 2) Record starting positions and wear

Mark each wheel position (LF/RF/LR/RR) and note wear patterns before moving anything.

### 3) Select pattern by drivetrain/tire constraints

For non-directional uniform-size sets, use a recognized pattern appropriate for drivetrain and setup.

On a typical NB rear-wheel-drive square setup, use **rearward cross**:
- rear tires move straight to the front on the same side,
- front tires move to rear on opposite sides.

> High-risk quote: "For vehicles that are rear wheel drive vehicles, the rearward cross pattern is recommended..." https://tires.bridgestone.com/en-us/learn/tire-maintenance/tire-rotation

### 4) Rotate wheels carefully

Swap wheels according to selected pattern, keeping mounting surfaces clean and wheels properly seated.

### 5) Tighten and torque correctly

Snug nuts in star pattern, lower vehicle, then final-torque in stages to spec from NB documentation.

### 6) Recheck after short drive

After brief driving/heat cycle, recheck lug torque and verify no vibration or pull.

Practical interval: recheck after **50-100 km (30-60 miles)**.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Use year-specific NB wheel-nut torque from factory manual data.
- Do not guess torque values; wheel security is safety-critical.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Steering remains stable with no new vibration.
- Wheel nuts remain at spec after short recheck interval.
- Wear trend should become more even over time.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources in this run align on why/when/how to rotate and on pattern-selection constraints.
- One universal NB rotation rule is not provided because owner modifications (directional tires, staggered sizes, wheel offsets) can invalidate standard cross-pattern guidance.
