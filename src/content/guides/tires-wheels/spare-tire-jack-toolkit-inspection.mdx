## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Many NB cars now have incomplete spare/jack kits. Confirming missing pieces before a roadside emergency is high-value maintenance.

> Warning: Always use stable, level ground and secure the vehicle before lifting; avoid changing a tire in unsafe traffic locations. https://www.bridgestonetire.com/learn/maintenance/how-to-change-a-flat-tire/

> High-risk quote: Do not place any part of your body under a vehicle supported only by an emergency jack. https://www.discounttire.com/learn/how-to-change-a-flat-tire

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact OEM jack points, spare pressure specs, and toolkit inventory should be confirmed by year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Tire pressure gauge
- Flashlight
- Clean rag
- Optional: torque wrench for home re-checks

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Spare tire (correct size/type for your NB setup)
- OEM or equivalent jack
- Lug wrench and any wheel-lock key if fitted

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Spare wheel/tool layouts can vary by market and trim; verify actual trunk kit contents against manual.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: aftermarket wheels/locks often add special key requirements not present in OEM toolkit.

> Community quote: r/Miata search shows recurring NB posts about missing jacks, missing wheel-lock keys, and uncertainty around safe jack points. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=nb%20spare%20tire%20jack&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Inventory all roadside essentials

Confirm presence/condition of:
- spare tire,
- jack,
- lug wrench,
- wheel-lock key (if applicable),
- tow eye/hook tools if applicable.

### 2) Check spare tire condition

- Measure pressure and set to spec.
- Inspect sidewalls for cracking/dry-rot.
- Check tread and age condition.

### 3) Inspect jack and handle mechanism

- Operate jack through short travel at home (no load first).
- Check threads, pivot points, and base for rust/bending.
- Ensure handle and extension tools fit and engage correctly.

### 4) Verify jack point knowledge

- Review OEM jack points for your NB.
- Do a dry run placing jack at correct point without lifting fully (safe environment).

### 5) Wheel fastener readiness check

- Confirm lug wrench fits your wheel nuts.
- Confirm wheel-lock key fits and is stored in known location.
- If using aftermarket wheels, verify tool compatibility now.

### 6) Stow and re-check interval

- Secure kit so nothing rattles or shifts.
- Recheck spare pressure and tool presence every service cycle or before long trips.

> Community confirmation quote: old.reddit NB spare/jack search context repeatedly highlights that owners discover missing or incompatible tools only during flats, supporting periodic pre-trip kit checks. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=nb+spare+tire+jack&restrict_sr=on

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Wheel lug torque after wheel change: follow NB year/VIN specification (FSM and wheel-lug guide).
- Spare tire pressure target: **year/VIN manual/FSM only**.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Spare pressure set and logged
- Jack and wrench confirmed functional
- Wheel-lock key present and tested (if equipped)
- Driver knows exact storage locations and jack points

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- NB spare/toolkit contents and spare pressure targets vary by market/trim and prior owner changes.
- Community evidence was partly search/snippet level.
- Therefore exact inventory/spec values are explicitly deferred to year/VIN documentation and actual car configuration.
