## Before You Start / Safety

- Perform torque checks on level ground with the vehicle stable.
- Use a calibrated torque wrench, not an impact gun for final tightening.
- Never guess torque by “feel” on aluminum wheels.

> Warning: "Do not tighten with a wrench" (until wheel is seated and pattern started), then complete in star pattern and final tightening procedure. https://www.firestonecompleteautocare.com/blog/tires/how-to-change-a-flat-tire/

## Required Tools

- Torque wrench
- Correct lug socket
- Breaker bar (if needed)
- Chalk/paint marker (optional for verification marks)

## Required Parts / Fluids

- None for inspection-only torque check
- Replacement studs/nuts if threads are damaged

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1/NB2 wheel hardware is often mixed with aftermarket wheels/seats (conical vs different seat designs).
- Always confirm lug seat compatibility and thread condition before final torque.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "Nb torque specs" https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=Nb+torque+specs&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm correct hardware and seating

Inspect each nut/stud for:
- damaged threads,
- incorrect seat type for wheel,
- debris/rust on wheel mounting surface.

### 2) Pre-seat wheel and start nuts by hand

Install nuts by hand first to avoid cross-threading.

### 3) Snug in star/criss-cross pattern

Before final torque, bring nuts down evenly in star sequence so the wheel seats flat.

> High-risk quote: "Work in a star-shaped or criss-cross pattern... it reduces the likelihood of a wobbly wheel." https://www.firestonecompleteautocare.com/blog/tires/how-to-change-a-flat-tire/

### 4) Final torque with torque wrench

Set wrench to NB-spec value from year-specific manual and tighten in star pattern.

### 5) Post-installation re-torque check

After a short drive/heat cycle, re-check lug torque.

> High-risk quote: "Regularly rotating your tires also gives you a good opportunity to visually inspect them..." (including wheel hardware checks during service intervals). https://tires.bridgestone.com/en-us/learn/tire-maintenance/tire-rotation

### 6) Escalate if torque won’t hold

If one nut repeatedly loosens or won’t torque correctly, inspect stud stretch, seat mismatch, and wheel face damage before further driving.

> Community confirmation quote: "Question about Lug Nuts" https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=Miata+lug+nuts+retorque&restrict_sr=1

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Use the exact NB year/VIN wheel-nut torque specification from the factory manual.
- This guide intentionally avoids publishing one universal number because wheel/hardware variations are common on NB cars.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No vibration or steering shimmy after wheel service.
- Re-torque confirmed after short drive.
- No visible stud or nut thread damage.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources in this run align on star-pattern tightening and re-check discipline.
- A single extractable universal NB lug-nut torque value was not published here due hardware and market variation; final value should be confirmed in year-specific FSM data and wheel manufacturer requirements.
