## Before You Start / Safety

- Check pressure when tires are cold whenever possible.
- Use a reliable gauge and avoid guessing by visual sidewall shape.
- Refit valve caps after adjustment to reduce dirt/moisture intrusion.

> Warning: "Vehicle manufacturers specify PSI ... assuming tires are cold. Tires are considered cold when the vehicle has been parked for three hours or more, or if the vehicle has been driven less than a mile." https://tires.bridgestone.com/en-us/learn/tire-maintenance/how-to-check-tire-pressure

## Required Tools

- Tire pressure gauge (digital or analog)
- Air source (compressor/pump)
- Pen/phone notes for each corner

## Required Parts / Fluids

- None for routine checks
- Replacement valve caps if missing/damaged

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 and NB2 recommended pressures can vary by wheel/tire size and market label.
- Always prioritize the vehicle label/manual for your specific setup.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "Best Tyre pressure for 195/50R15?" https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB+tire+pressure&restrict_sr=1

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Find target pressure first

Use driver-door-jamb label and owner/manual data for front/rear targets before measuring.

### 2) Measure all four tires (cold)

- Remove cap, press gauge firmly on valve, record PSI.
- Repeat all corners, including the one that “looks fine.”

### 3) Adjust to target

- Add air in small bursts.
- Recheck after each burst.
- If overfilled, bleed down and re-measure.

### 4) Hot-tire correction (if unavoidable)

If you must set pressure with warm tires, re-check again when cold and finalize to cold spec.

> High-risk quote: "If it is necessary to adjust inflation pressure when tires are 'hot', set their pressure to 4 psi ... above the recommended cold inflation pressure. Recheck ... when the tires are cold." https://tires.bridgestone.com/en-us/learn/tire-maintenance/how-to-check-tire-pressure

### 5) Monthly routine and before long trips

Make pressure checks periodic (at least monthly) and before highway trips or major weather swings.

> High-risk quote: "Tire inflation is an important factor to ensure your safety ..." https://www.michelinman.com/auto/auto-tips-and-advice/tire-pressure

> High-risk quote: "Regularly checking your tire pressure is the best way to ensure your tires never dip far below the optimal PSI." https://tires.bridgestone.com/en-us/learn/tire-maintenance/how-to-check-tire-pressure

### 6) Investigate repeat loss

If one tire repeatedly drops faster than others, inspect for puncture/valve/rim leaks instead of repeated top-offs only.

> Community confirmation quote: "Tire pressure, 44 psi all around from dealer. Just noticed in the app." https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB+tire+pressure&restrict_sr=1

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Not applicable for routine pressure checks.
- For wheel removal/lug torque, refer to dedicated lug torque guide.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Reconfirm all four pressures after adjustment.
- Ensure valve caps are installed.
- Confirm steering feel and straight tracking on short drive.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources in this run agree strongly on cold-tire method and periodic checks.
- One universal NB pressure number is not published here because target PSI depends on wheel/tire size and year/market label; final values should come from your specific vehicle label/manual.
