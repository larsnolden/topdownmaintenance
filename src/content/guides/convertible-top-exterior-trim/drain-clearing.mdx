## Before You Start / Safety

- Park on level ground and let the car cool.
- Use flexible, non-sharp tools first (brush/cable), not rigid pointed metal.
- If drains remain blocked, stop forcing tools to avoid tube disconnection or puncture.

> Warning: "Do not operate the convertible top while moving." https://www.youcanic.com/convertible-maintenance-tips/

## Required Tools

- Soft flexible drain brush (e.g., trombone/hood drain brush style)
- Flashlight
- Clean water bottle for flow testing
- Trim tool / panel fastener tool (if lower access is required)
- Microfiber towels for interior moisture checks

## Required Parts / Fluids

- No parts for routine clearing
- If damaged/disconnected: replacement drain tube/grommet components as required

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 and NB2 share the same basic soft-top water path concept: rain rail → side drains → outlet.
- Many cars now have aged or previously repaired drain tubes; be conservative with tool stiffness.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "This is on a mk2 btw..." https://www.pistonheads.com/gassing/topic.asp?t=798737

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm symptoms and baseline flow

Common clues include damp carpet behind seats, musty cabin smell, and visible standing water in drain areas after rain.

### 2) Access upper drain inlets

Lower/open top enough to expose drain entry points behind seat-belt tower area. Remove loose debris by hand/soft brush.

### 3) Clear with gentle tool technique

Feed flexible cleaner through the drain path with light pressure and slight rotation. Avoid ramming the tool through resistance.

> High-risk quote: "Hood drains can be a bit fragile, the rubber tube may crack/puncture." https://www.pistonheads.com/gassing/topic.asp?t=798737

### 4) Consider direction and flap sensitivity

Community experience shows one-way flap areas and joints can be disturbed by aggressive poking; proceed gently and re-check from both accessible ends when needed.

> Community confirmation quote: "I think I probably dislodged/pushed it all apart when doing a monthly 'check my drain tubes are clear!'" https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/guide-drain-tube-repair-and-bonus-drainage-info.250370/

### 5) Water-flow test immediately

Pour a small amount of clean water into each drain channel. Confirm it exits quickly underneath without backing up.

### 6) Escalate to inspection/repair if still slow

If flow remains poor, inspect lower tube/grommet condition and seating. A disconnected or damaged tube will require repair rather than repeated rodding.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Not applicable for routine drain clearing.
- If trim/hardware is removed during inspection, use year-specific NB service references for any re-tightening values.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Repeat water-flow test on both sides.
- Inspect rear carpet area after next rain/wash.
- No sloshing sounds or recurring moisture should remain.
