## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

NB cars can use different cam signal hardware approaches depending on year/engine/market. Do not assume NA-era CAS procedures are directly transferable.

> Warning: Work with ignition OFF and battery disconnected as appropriate before unplugging sensor/CAS connectors. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

> High-risk quote: Crank/cam position signals are core timing inputs for ignition and injection, so sensor or wiring faults can produce no-start or intermittent stalling. https://www.aa1car.com/library/crank_sensors.htm

> Manual-reference quote: NB service info is split by year groups (1999-2001 and 2005), so exact sensor type, location, and O-ring/seal procedure must be confirmed by year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Basic hand tools and picks for connector release
- Multimeter and scan tool (recommended)
- Clean rags/degreaser for leak tracing
- New O-ring/seal where serviceable and specified

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct cam sensor/CAS unit for exact NB variant
- Correct O-ring/seal (if separately serviceable)
- Small amount of clean oil for O-ring installation lubrication

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Cam signal architecture and sensor packaging can differ from later NB2.
- Confirm whether you are servicing a sensor body only or housing with seal interface.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- NB2/VVT-era cars can introduce additional component-layout complexity.
- Confirm connector compatibility before install; catalog matches can still be wrong.

> Community quote: Miata community reports show NB cam/cas replacement confusion around connector compatibility, with “correct” listed parts sometimes not matching installed harnesses. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=NB+cam+sensor+CAS&restrict_sr=on

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm fault context before replacing parts

- Pull codes and freeze-frame.
- Note hot-start/no-start/intermittent stall behavior.
- Rule out obvious coil/CKP/wiring issues first.

### 2) Confirm leak source before O-ring service

- Clean area around cam sensor/CAS/housing.
- Run engine briefly and trace fresh oil path.
- Differentiate CAS/sensor seal leak from valve-cover or nearby seepage.

### 3) Verify sensor/connector type before removal

- Compare installed connector keying to replacement part.
- Verify pin count and harness indexing.

### 4) Remove sensor/CAS carefully

- Disconnect harness without pulling wires.
- Remove retaining fasteners and withdraw unit gently.
- Protect mating surfaces and avoid twisting damage.

### 5) Service O-ring/seal (if applicable)

- Replace with correct size/material O-ring.
- Lightly lubricate before install.
- Seat squarely; avoid pinching or rolling seal.

> Community confirmation quote: MX5Nutz CAS/O-ring discussions highlight installation-angle/indexing sensitivity and leak recurrence when sealing surfaces or engagement position are not set correctly. https://duckduckgo.com/html/?q=site:mx5nutz.com+NB+cam+sensor+CAS

### 6) Reinstall, verify sync, and recheck for leaks

- Refit unit and connector.
- Start engine and verify stable operation.
- Recheck for oil seepage after warm-up and after a short drive.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Sensor/CAS fastener torque and sealing details are year/VIN specific.
- Use exact FSM values for your NB configuration.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No recurring cam/correlation/no-start faults after drive cycle.
- Stable idle/hot restart behavior.
- No fresh oil around serviced sensor/CAS area.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources agree on diagnosis-first and connector-match verification, but detailed NB market-specific sensor architecture varies.
- Several direct how-to pages were unavailable/404 in this run; this article uses accessible references and explicitly defers exact pin-level thresholds and torque specs to year/VIN FSM.
