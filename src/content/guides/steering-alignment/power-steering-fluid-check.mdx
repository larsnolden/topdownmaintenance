## Before You Start / Safety

- Park on level ground.
- Keep fluid away from belts and hot exhaust parts.
- If steering effort suddenly increases or fluid sprays/leaks, stop driving and inspect for leaks before continued use.

> Warning: "If there isn't enough fluid, steering becomes more difficult and either the pump or rack and pinion could be damaged without fluid to cushion them." https://www.wikihow.com/Check-and-Add-Power-Steering-Fluid

## Required Tools

- Clean rag/paper towel
- Small funnel
- Correct fluid (per manual/spec)
- Flashlight

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Power steering fluid/ATF compatible with NB requirements (verify against your year manual and product label)

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB power steering systems are hydraulic; reservoir cap and dipstick condition matter (a loose cap can contribute to fluid loss/mess).
- Verify fluid compatibility against Mazda spec language for your year/manual.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "The service manual I have for the car (for MY1999) says 'ATF M-III or equivalent (eg. Dexron II).'" https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=549067

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Locate reservoir and identify level markings

NB reservoir level is checked at the power steering reservoir using cap/dipstick or reservoir marks (depending on design).

> High-risk quote: "The video above shows you the steps needed to check the power steering fluid level in your 2001 Mazda Miata... Your Miata should be parked on a level surface and your 1.8 liter engine should be cold to the touch." https://www.carcarekiosk.com/video/2001_Mazda_Miata_LS_1.8L_4_Cyl./power_steering_fluid/check_fluid_level

### 2) Clean around cap before opening

Wipe dirt from the cap and reservoir neck first to reduce contamination risk.

### 3) Read fluid level correctly

- If dipstick type: remove, wipe, reinsert, and re-read.
- If marked reservoir: compare against MIN/MAX or COLD/HOT marks per manual method.

> High-risk quote: "On some cars, the power-steering fluid level can only be checked accurately after the engine has run for a brief period... On still other cars, there may be 'Min' and 'Max' lines..." https://www.wikihow.com/Check-and-Add-Power-Steering-Fluid

### 4) Top up in small amounts if low

Add compatible fluid gradually, re-check, and avoid overfilling.

> High-risk quote: "If the power steering fluid level in your Miata is low, fill it up to the maximum line with power steering fluid and check it again in a week to determine if you have a leak." https://www.carcarekiosk.com/video/2001_Mazda_Miata_LS_1.8L_4_Cyl./power_steering_fluid/check_fluid_level

### 5) Assess fluid condition and leak clues

If fluid is repeatedly low, dark/contaminated, or you have pump noise/hard steering, investigate leaks and consider fluid service rather than repeated top-offs.

### 6) Refit cap securely and verify steering feel

Confirm cap is fully seated. A loose dipstick/cap can contribute to fluid escaping or being thrown around the engine bay.

> Community confirmation quote: "The dip stick of my power steering fluid was propped open... apparently the pressure in the system popped the cap open and seemed to leak fluid..." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=720029

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- This guide is level-check/top-up focused; no universal NB capacity value is published here.
- Capacity can vary with service state (dry fill vs top-up). Use year/VIN-specific factory manual data for exact capacities.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Steering effort should be smooth at parking speeds.
- Recheck level after a short drive and again within ~1 week.
- If level drops again, inspect hose/reservoir/rack areas for leaks.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Publicly accessible sources agree on core process (level surface, clean cap area, incremental top-up, leak follow-up), but fluid-spec naming differs by market/year and product labeling.
- Final fluid choice should follow your exact NB year manual specification and bottle compatibility statement.
