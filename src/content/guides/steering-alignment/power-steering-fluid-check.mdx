## Before You Start / Safety

- Park on level ground.
- Engine off for cold-level checks; keep hands clear of belts/pulleys if checking hot marks.
- Clean around cap before opening so dirt does not enter the hydraulic system.

## Required Tools

- Clean lint-free rag
- Small funnel
- Flashlight
- 250 ml measuring cup or syringe bottle (for controlled top-up)
- Compatible ATF (see fluid spec below)

## Required Parts / Fluids

For many NB references (including 1999 Miata service-manual language discussed by owners), power steering fluid is specified as:

- **ATF M-III or equivalent (e.g., Dexron II-type ATF)**

Practical modern equivalents commonly used by owners:

- Dexron III/Mercon labeled ATF
- Multi-vehicle ATF explicitly listing Dexron II/III compatibility

Do **not** mix unknown fluid types blindly; if current fluid is unknown/contaminated, perform a full fluid exchange.

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 and NB2 both use hydraulic power steering (not electric assist).
- Reservoir is in the engine bay near front accessory side; cap has an attached dipstick on many cars.
- Some reservoirs use COLD/HOT ranges; check against fluid temperature.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Find the reservoir and identify markings

Open hood and locate the power steering reservoir (small round reservoir with screw cap and dipstick). Check whether your cap shows **COLD** and **HOT** range marks.

### 2) Clean the cap area first

Wipe the cap and surrounding reservoir neck so grit does not drop into fluid.

### 3) Read level correctly

- Unscrew cap/dipstick.
- Wipe dipstick clean.
- Reinsert fully, remove, and read level.
- Compare to COLD or HOT range based on fluid temperature.

### 4) Top up in small steps

- Add ATF in small increments (about 50-100 ml at a time).
- Re-check after each addition.
- Stop at upper mark; overfilling can aerate fluid and cause foaming/noise.

### 5) Check for leak clues

If fluid was low, inspect:

- Reservoir cap seal and neck
- Pump area and hose crimps
- Steering rack boots for wet ATF
- Undertray/ground after parking

### 6) Functional check

Start engine, turn wheel lock-to-lock slowly 2-3 times, then re-check level. Steering should feel smooth with no whining.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- This is a check/top-up guide; capacity varies by how much fluid was lost and whether system was drained.
- For full-system refill numbers, verify against your exact year FSM.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No pump whine at idle parking maneuvers
- No foam/bubbles on dipstick
- Level stable at next weekly check
- No fresh ATF traces around pump/rack/hoses
