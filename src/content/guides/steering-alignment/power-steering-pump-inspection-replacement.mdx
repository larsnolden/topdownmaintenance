## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Power steering pump noise is not always a bad pump. Low fluid, aeration, wrong fluid, belt issues, or rack problems can mimic pump failure.

> Warning: "Use jack stands to support the vehicle if needed." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-a-brake-master-cylinder

> High-risk quote: Low or contaminated PS fluid can quickly damage expensive steering components. https://www.familyhandyman.com/article/how-to-check-power-steering-fluid/

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so pump removal sequence, belt tension procedure, and fluid spec must be confirmed by exact year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Distinguish pump whine from aeration/fluid issues

Check first:
- fluid level and condition,
- foam/bubbles in reservoir,
- fluid specification correctness,
- leaks at hoses/fittings.

Do not condemn pump until fluid and air-ingress checks are complete.

### 2) Check belt-driven causes

Inspect belt condition/tension and pulley alignment.

Squeal that changes with steering load may come from belt slip, not internal pump failure.

### 3) Confirm symptom severity

Stronger evidence for pump replacement includes persistent whine with correct fluid/level, metal contamination pattern, or confirmed shaft/seal failure.

### 4) Pump replacement (if confirmed)

- Drain/remove as much old fluid as practical.
- Remove belt and hose connections with spill control.
- Transfer pulley/hardware as required.
- Install replacement pump and reconnect lines using new seals where specified.

### 5) Refill and de-aerate system

- Fill with correct fluid.
- Cycle steering slowly lock-to-lock without holding at full lock.
- Keep reservoir above minimum to prevent fresh air ingestion.

### 6) Post-repair verification

- Check for leaks at all line joints.
- Recheck fluid level after warm-up.
- Confirm reduced/no whine and normal assist.

> Community confirmation quote: old.reddit NB power-steering-pump search context repeatedly points users to deeper Miata.net/MX5Nutz troubleshooting when whine remains after fluid work, supporting stepwise diagnosis before parts replacement. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=nb+power+steering+pump&restrict_sr=on

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No persistent steering whine after de-aeration
- No foaming in reservoir
- No fluid leaks at pump/hoses/fittings
- Stable steering assist at parking and low-speed maneuvers
