## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)** only.

Steering rack work is safety-critical. Poor assembly or incomplete bleed/alignment can cause unstable steering behavior.

> Warning: "Use jack stands to support the vehicle if needed." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-a-brake-master-cylinder

> High-risk quote: Low or contaminated power steering fluid can damage expensive steering components quickly. https://www.familyhandyman.com/article/how-to-check-power-steering-fluid/

> Manual-reference quote: Mellens indexes NB-era manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so rack removal sequence, hydraulic line torque, and steering geometry setup must be confirmed by exact year/VIN. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Floor jack + rated jack stands
- Socket/wrench set and line wrenches
- Tie-rod separator tools
- Torque wrench
- Fluid catch containers and absorbents
- Steering wheel locking/centering strategy

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct NB steering rack assembly or rebuild kit (matched to RHD/LHD and year)
- New rack boots/clamps if required
- New line sealing washers/O-rings where specified
- Correct power steering fluid for your NB year/market

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Confirm rack ratio/mounting and line fittings before ordering.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Same caution: prior steering swaps are common; verify actual installed rack and tie-rod hardware.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm rack fault before replacement

Differentiate rack failure from:
- hose/line leaks,
- pump whine/aeration,
- tie-rod wear,
- subframe/mount issues.

Rack replacement is justified when leakage/play is confirmed at rack body/seals or internal wear is evident.

### 2) Baseline steering geometry references

- Center steering wheel and mark position.
- Record tie-rod length/thread exposure both sides.
- Mark any adjustable hardware baseline positions.

These are reassembly aids only—**alignment is still mandatory**.

### 3) Drain and disconnect hydraulic system

- Drain/contain PS fluid.
- Disconnect lines with line wrenches.
- Cap open lines/ports to minimize contamination.

### 4) Disconnect steering/tie-rod interfaces

- Separate outer tie rods from knuckles.
- Disconnect steering column coupler per FSM.
- Remove rack mounts and extract rack assembly carefully.

### 5) Rebuild vs replacement decision point

- **Complete replacement** is typically safer/faster when seal wear and internal rack condition are unknown.
- **Rebuild** only if tooling, clean assembly conditions, and verified service data are available.

### 6) Install rack and reconnect system

- Install rack with mounts in FSM sequence.
- Reconnect steering shaft coupling with correct orientation.
- Reconnect hydraulic lines with new seals where required.
- Set tie-rod baseline length/position.

### 7) Refill, de-aerate, and align

- Refill with correct fluid.
- Bleed/de-aerate using FSM method (slow lock-to-lock, no prolonged full-lock holds).
- Perform full alignment after any rack/tie-rod disturbance.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No leaks at rack body, boots, line fittings, or pump connections
- No reservoir foaming after de-aeration
- Smooth steering effort, no binding through full lock range
- Steering wheel centered and alignment confirmed
