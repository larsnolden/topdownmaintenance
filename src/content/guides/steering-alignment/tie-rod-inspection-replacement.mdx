## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on level ground with the car securely supported.
- Keep the steering unlocked only when needed for checks.
- Any severe tie-rod play is safety-critical; avoid road use until repaired.

> Warning: "Make sure your car is jacked securely. The vehicle must be on frame stands when jacked up and you are working on the vehicle." https://www.wikihow.com/Check-Ball-Joints

## Required Tools

- Jack and axle stands
- Wheel chocks
- Spanners/sockets
- Pry bar
- Tie-rod end separator or shock-release hammer technique
- Paint marker (for jam nut/index marks)
- Torque wrench

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Outer tie rod end(s) for your NB steering setup
- Cotter pin(s) and locking hardware if applicable
- Penetrant

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB steering/suspension combinations vary by year and prior modifications, so verify part dimensions and thread style before install.
- If you replaced multiple front-end parts (racks/inner rods/ends), initial toe can be far from drivable even if parts are installed correctly.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "I replaced all of the ball joints and tie-rod ends. Now, I have massive toe-out." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=627428

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Inspect for wear before disassembly

Check each outer end for:
- torn/dry-rotted boots,
- grease loss,
- free play/noise during wheel shake tests,
- steering shake or uneven tire wear symptoms.

### 2) Set a baseline toe reference

Before removal, mark jam-nut position and count turns during end removal. This is only a baseline to get close enough for transport to alignment.

### 3) Separate tie-rod end from knuckle safely

- Loosen the tapered-stud nut but leave a few threads engaged.
- Release taper with proper separator or controlled shock method.

> High-risk quote: "leave the nut on about 3 threads to protect the threads and to hold it in place during removal." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/tie-rod-end-replacement

### 4) Transfer setup to new tie-rod end

Match old/new part length and thread direction. Install to the marked baseline (or same turn count), then seat taper in knuckle.

### 5) Tighten hardware and secure locking features

Torque the stud nut to the correct year/VIN spec and install cotter/locking method where required.

If the stud spins before taper seats, apply seating force to the joint while tightening.

> Community confirmation quote: "Use a ball joint splitter to clamp it together...?" https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=491127

### 6) Mandatory alignment and verification

Repaired tie rods require a proper alignment. Baseline marks/turn counts are not a final alignment method.

> High-risk quote: "there is no torque setting for this nut ... now the vehicle is ready for alignment." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/tie-rod-end-replacement

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Final torque values for tie-rod stud nuts and related hardware must be taken from your exact NB year/VIN factory manual.
- This guide intentionally avoids publishing one universal torque number due package/year and hardware variability.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Steering wheel returns smoothly and no clunk at low speed.
- No looseness at tie-rod joint after recheck.
- Vehicle receives full front alignment after replacement.
- Recheck fastener security after short shakedown.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources available in this run consistently support process controls (safe support, protecting threads, baseline marking, and alignment after replacement).
- A single complete NB-wide torque table and part-length matrix was not extractable from public sources in this environment; final values should be confirmed in year/VIN-specific FSM documentation.
