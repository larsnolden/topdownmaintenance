## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on flat ground.
- If lifting the car for suspension checks, support it securely on stands.
- A driveway check is diagnostic only; final settings require calibrated alignment equipment.

> Warning: "Make sure your car is jacked securely. The vehicle must be on frame stands when jacked up and you are working on the vehicle." https://www.wikihow.com/Check-Ball-Joints

## Required Tools

- Tire pressure gauge
- Tread depth gauge (or careful visual tread comparison)
- Tape measure/string (optional baseline toe check)
- Notepad for left/right measurements and symptoms

## Required Parts / Fluids

- None for inspection-only checks
- If correction is needed: alignment service and any worn steering/suspension parts found during pre-check

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB alignment targets vary by year, trim, wheel/tire setup, and suspension modifications.
- Treat any home baseline method as a way to make the car transport-safe to an alignment shop, not as final spec adjustment.

> Manual-reference quote: "Welcome to Mazda Miata Factory Service Manuals" https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

> Community quote: "I replaced all of the ball joints and tie-rod ends. Now, I have massive toe-out." https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=627428

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Start with tire and steering baselines

- Set tire pressures to your chosen baseline first.
- Confirm wheel bearings, tie rods, and ball joints are not loose before judging alignment.

### 2) Road symptom triage

On a level road, note:
- pull/drift,
- off-center steering wheel when driving straight,
- wandering/instability,
- steering that feels inconsistent left vs right.

### 3) Tread-wear pattern check

Compare inner/center/outer wear across both front tires (and rear tires for thrust-angle clues). Uneven edge wear is a common alignment trigger.

> High-risk quote: "A wheel alignment is the process of adjusting the angles of your vehicle’s steering and suspension components back to original specifications." https://www.lesschwab.com/alignment

### 4) Basic garage toe sanity check (optional)

Use string or equivalent reference method to compare front-vs-rear tire edge distance on each side. This is a coarse check to identify large toe errors only.

> Community confirmation quote: "Center the steering wheel ... Use a string long enough to reach the center of both tire treads as reference" https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=219766

### 5) Decide if immediate professional alignment is required

Book alignment promptly if you observe:
- significant pull or off-center wheel,
- rapid/uneven tire wear,
- recent steering/suspension part replacement,
- obvious toe error from baseline checks.

> High-risk quote: "now the vehicle is ready for alignment." https://www.2carpros.com/articles/tie-rod-end-replacement

### 6) Post-alignment verification

After shop alignment:
- steering wheel should be centered,
- tracking should be stable on flat road,
- no new pull under steady throttle/cruise.

Recheck tire wear after several hundred kilometers to confirm improvement.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- This guide is inspection-focused and does not publish universal NB alignment numbers.
- Final camber/caster/toe targets and any related fastener torques should be taken from year/VIN-specific NB service data (or your chosen motorsport setup sheet where applicable).

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Steering wheel centered after alignment.
- No abnormal pull on level road.
- Tire wear trend stabilizes.
- If pull persists, re-check tire condition/pressure, brake drag, and suspension play.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources in this run strongly agree on symptom-based triggers and the need for machine-based final alignment.
- One complete, directly extractable NB1/NB2 factory alignment-spec table was not reliably available in this environment; confirm exact targets in the relevant year manual.
