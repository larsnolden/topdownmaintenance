## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on a fully cool engine.
- Disconnect battery negative if you are removing ignition components.
- Keep debris out of the valvetrain area.
- Do **not** overtighten valve cover fasteners; stripped threads and warped covers are common DIY failure points.

## Required Tools

- Metric socket set and ratchet
- Torque wrench capable of low torque values
- Plastic scraper / non-marring pick
- Brake cleaner or degreaser and lint-free rags
- Small amount of RTV/silicone where the service manual calls for it

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Valve (cam) cover gasket set for your exact NB engine/version
- Optional spark plug well seals (if included with your gasket kit)
- Optional PCV valve/hoses if brittle or cracked

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1 (1998-2000) and NB2 (2001-2005, incl. many VVT 1.8s) share the same maintenance concept but differ in surrounding hardware and exact part numbers.
- Confirm by VIN/year and engine code before ordering.

> Manual-reference quote: BOFI’s workshop-manual index lists NB-specific engine manuals (“99-00 for the bp4w 1.8” and “01-05 for the vvt engine”). (Retrieved 2026-02-26) https://bofiracing.com/blog/mx5-miata-workshop-manuals/

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm leak source first

Clean the area and verify the leak is from the valve cover perimeter/plug-well area rather than a higher or adjacent source.

### 2) Remove ignition/obstructing components and valve cover

- Label anything you disconnect.
- Remove fasteners evenly.
- Lift the cover carefully to avoid nicking sealing surfaces.

### 3) Clean mating surfaces

- Remove old gasket material gently.
- Degrease cover channel and head mating surface.
- Keep debris out of the engine.

### 4) Install new gasket and seals

- Seat gasket fully in the valve cover groove.
- Apply sealant only at the specific corners/joints required by the service procedure.

> Community quote: “don’t forget to use high temp silicone for those sharp bends/corners … And use OEM Valve gasket…” (Miata.net NB thread, retrieved 2026-02-26) https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=442560

### 5) Refit cover and torque in stages

- Snug all fasteners by hand first.
- Tighten in small stages and sequence from center-out per manual guidance.
- Re-check that gasket hasn’t rolled/pinched during seating.

### 6) Reassemble and verify

- Refit ignition parts/hoses.
- Start engine, inspect for leaks at idle and after a full heat cycle.

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- Use your NB workshop manual for exact valve cover fastener torque by engine variant.
- This guide intentionally avoids publishing an unverified single torque number because accessible public sources in this environment were inconsistent or incomplete.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No fresh oil around cover perimeter or plug wells after warm-up.
- No misfire from oil contamination in plug wells.
- Reinspect after 1-2 drives.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Some detailed step references (e.g., miata.net garage how-to mirror pages) are intermittently blocked by anti-bot protections in this environment.
- Community advice differs on reusing old gaskets. Conservative approach is to replace with high-quality/OEM-equivalent gasket when opened.

> Community quote (reuse caution): “the downside of reusing the gasket is that it leaks and then you'd need to buy a new gasket, and then do the labor again.” (Miata.net NB thread, retrieved 2026-02-26) https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=643394
