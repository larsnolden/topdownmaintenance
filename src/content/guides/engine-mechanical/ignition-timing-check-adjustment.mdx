## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005)**.

For many NB setups, ignition timing is ECU-managed rather than routinely adjustable like earlier distributor-era cars. So this is primarily a **check-and-verify** guide unless your exact year/market/manual explicitly allows a base-timing adjustment procedure.

> Warning: Keep hands, sleeves, and test leads clear of belts/fans while the engine is running during timing-light checks. https://www.garagetooled.com/auto/how-to-use-a-timing-light/

> Manual-reference quote: NB service documentation is split by year groups (1999-2001 and 2005 manuals), so timing-check and any adjustment criteria must be verified against your exact year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Inductive timing light
- Basic hand tools for covers/inspection access
- White paint marker/chalk for timing marks (optional)
- Shop light and mirror (optional)
- FSM or equivalent year/VIN-correct service information

## Required Parts / Fluids

- None for check-only work
- If doing any ignition-system follow-up: plugs/coils/wires as needed by diagnosis

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Community discussion commonly reports NB/Mk2 timing as ECU-controlled and not a simple CAS-rotation adjustment like earlier NA procedures.
- If your specific market variant has a supported base-setting check routine, follow FSM exactly.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Community and owner discussions strongly describe NB2/Mk2.5 timing as ECU-managed and effectively non-adjustable without non-standard modifications.
- Treat any “slot bracket / trigger wheel” mod as custom, not standard maintenance.

> Community quote: “The timing is controlled by the ECU, and cannot be set.” (NB/Mk2 discussion) https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/am-i-dumb-cant-find-where-to-adjust-mk2-1-8-timing.151373/

> Community confirmation quote: MX-5Nutz timing-mod discussions also distinguish Mk1-style base timing tweaks from Mk2 systems and warn that Mk2 uses a different control strategy. https://www.mx5nutz.com/threads/guide-14-degree-ignition-timing-mod.122153/

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm this is a timing check request, not a symptom guess

Before timing work, confirm current complaints (rough idle, pinging, low power, hot start issues) are not caused by vacuum leaks, fueling, or mechanical timing errors.

### 2) Warm engine to normal operating temperature

- Run engine until fully warm.
- Stable idle is important before interpreting timing-light readings.

> High-risk quote: Timing checks are performed with the engine at normal running condition and timing marks viewed with a strobe at idle. https://www.scegliauto.com/en/video/mazda/tutorial/2867/

### 3) Locate and clean timing marks

- Identify crank pulley and reference pointer marks.
- Clean and highlight marks if needed for visibility.
- Connect timing light power and #1 lead pickup per tool instructions.

### 4) Run the check

- Idle engine and aim timing light at marks.
- Observe timing stability, not just one flash snapshot.
- Record reading and idle condition.

### 5) Determine whether adjustment is factory-applicable

- If FSM says check-only: stop at verification and continue diagnosis elsewhere.
- If FSM includes a permitted base-setting procedure for your variant: follow that specific routine only.
- Do **not** apply NA distributor-style adjustment assumptions to NB cars.

### 6) Re-verify after any permitted change

- Recheck at idle and after brief throttle blips.
- Confirm no new drivability issues.

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- No universal NB torque values are published here because this job is usually check-only on NB platforms.
- If your FSM allows an adjustment step requiring loosen/tighten hardware, use year/VIN-specific torque values from that FSM.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Idle quality remains stable.
- No misfire or knock introduced after work.
- No warning lights triggered after road test.
- Reconfirm timing reading repeatability if symptoms persist.

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources are aligned on **method** (timing-light check) but mixed on exact “adjustability” across all NB market variants.
- Community evidence strongly indicates most NB configurations are ECU-controlled and not intended for routine manual base-timing adjustment.
- Therefore this guide prioritizes conservative check/verify workflow and FSM-first decisions.
