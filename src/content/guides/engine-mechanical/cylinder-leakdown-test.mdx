## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

A leak-down test is a follow-up diagnostic when compression readings are uneven, low, or suspicious. It does **not** replace basic mechanical timing and ignition/fueling checks.

> Warning: A leak-down test uses compressed air; secure the drivetrain and keep hands/tools clear because the engine can rotate if the piston is not truly at TDC on compression. https://www.autozone.com/diy/tools/how-to-perform-a-cylinder-leak-down-test

> High-risk quote: “It’s critical that the piston is at the top of the compression stroke when both intake and exhaust valves are closed.” https://www.speedwaymotors.com/the-toolbox/how-to-do-an-engine-cylinder-leak-down-test-and-interpreting-results/145764

> Manual-reference quote: NB service procedures are organized by year groups (1999-2001 and 2005 manual sets), so final threshold interpretation and follow-up decisions should be checked against year/VIN FSM references. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Cylinder leak-down tester (dual-gauge preferred)
- Air compressor with regulator
- Spark plug socket + ratchet/extensions
- Breaker bar/socket for crank rotation
- Basic hand tools and good lighting
- Notebook/app for per-cylinder readings

## Required Parts / Fluids

- No replacement parts required for testing itself
- Optional: spark plugs (if due), anti-seize only if plug maker/manual allows

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

### NB1 (1998-2000)
- Test method is unchanged: warm engine, each cylinder at TDC on compression, add regulated air, record leakage and leak path.

### NB2 (2001-2005)
- Method is also unchanged.
- VVT-equipped cars still use the same leak-down logic; interpretation must consider overall engine condition and known running symptoms.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Prepare and warm the engine

- Bring engine to operating temperature where possible.
- Shut down, remove ignition/fuel enable as needed, and remove spark plugs.

> High-risk quote: Warming first improves seal realism because ring sealing behavior changes with temperature. https://ricksfreeautorepairadvice.com/how-to-perform-a-cylinder-leakdown-test/

### 2) Set cylinder #1 at TDC on compression stroke

- Rotate crank by hand.
- Confirm you are at **compression TDC** (both valves closed for that cylinder).
- Lock/hold position carefully before adding shop air.

### 3) Connect tester and set reference pressure

- Install hose adapter in plug hole.
- Connect tester and regulated air supply.
- Zero/calibrate tester per tool instructions.

### 4) Apply air and record leakage percentage

- Add air gradually.
- Record leakage % for that cylinder.
- Listen for where air escapes:
  - intake tract → intake valve sealing issue
  - exhaust tailpipe/manifold → exhaust valve sealing issue
  - oil filler/dipstick/crankcase breathers → ring/cylinder leakage
  - cooling system bubbling/noise → head gasket/crack suspicion

> High-risk quote: Leak-down is used to identify *where* compression is escaping, not just whether compression is low. https://www.autozone.com/diy/tools/how-to-perform-a-cylinder-leak-down-test

### 5) Repeat for cylinders 2-4 with same setup discipline

- Reposition each piston to compression TDC before testing.
- Keep test pressure and process consistent across all cylinders.

> High-risk quote: Consistent supply pressure and repeatable method are essential for meaningful cylinder-to-cylinder comparison. https://ricksfreeautorepairadvice.com/how-to-perform-a-cylinder-leakdown-test/

### 6) Interpret results by pattern, not one number alone

- Small leakage with even cylinder spread generally indicates healthy sealing for age/mileage.
- One cylinder markedly worse than others usually indicates localized valve/ring/head-gasket fault.
- Multiple cylinders high can indicate broader wear or shared issue.

> Community quote: Miata community leak-down discussions commonly report that one outlier cylinder with very high loss changes diagnosis from “tune-up issue” toward mechanical repair planning. https://old.reddit.com/r/Miata/search?q=leakdown&restrict_sr=on

> Community confirmation quote: Community posts also show leak-down results are most useful when paired with symptoms (hard start/misfire/power loss) rather than read in isolation. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=leakdown&restrict_sr=1

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Not applicable for leak-down measurement itself.
- Spark plug reinstall torque after test should follow year/VIN FSM and plug manufacturer guidance.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Reinstall plugs and connectors correctly.
- Confirm normal start and idle after test.
- If major leakage is found, avoid hard driving until root cause plan is made.
- Use leak-down + compression + symptom history to choose next step (valve work, ring diagnosis, head-gasket testing, etc.).

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Public sources differ on exact “good/acceptable/bad” percentage cutoffs, and those values depend on tester design, pressure baseline, temperature, and engine age.
- This guide therefore emphasizes **cylinder balance/trend** and leak path location over one universal pass/fail number.
- Final interpretation for repair decisions should be cross-checked against year/VIN FSM data and measured symptoms.
