## Before You Start / Safety

- Work on a cool engine in a ventilated area.
- Wear nitrile gloves and eye protection.
- Use throttle-body/carb-intake cleaner only as directed by product label.
- Keep ignition sources away from solvent vapors.

> Warning: “This should be an outdoor place with good ventilation because the substances you shall use are volatile.” https://rxmechanic.com/how-to-clean-throttle-body/

> High-risk quote: “Be careful not to let any parts in the throttle body spill.” https://rxmechanic.com/how-to-clean-throttle-body/

## Required Tools

- Screwdrivers / hose-clamp driver
- Clean lint-free cloths
- Soft brush (nylon)
- Throttle body cleaner
- Small inspection light

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Throttle-body cleaner (sensor-safe type preferred)
- Optional replacement intake hose clamp(s) or gasket(s) if damaged during removal

## Model-specific notes (NB1 vs NB2)

- NB1/NB2 are cable-throttle cars (not later drive-by-wire logic), so cleaning workflow is generally straightforward.
- Access and hose routing can vary with stock vs aftermarket intake plumbing.

> Manual-reference quote: “The Manuals are in PDF format. You can use Adobe Reader to read or print them.” https://www.mellens.net/mazda/

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Baseline check first

Confirm symptoms before cleaning (idle dip, unstable idle, off-idle hesitation). If there is a hard fault/CEL, diagnose that separately instead of assuming deposits are the only cause.

> Community quote: “I’m having some idling issues … vibration and RPMs drop down and eventually find their correct idle.” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=484366

### 2) Access the throttle body

Remove the intake duct between airbox/MAF side and throttle body so the throttle plate and bore are visible.

> High-risk quote: “Locate the air intake tube and loosen the clamps and mounting bolts to remove it.” https://www.2carpros.com/articles/throttle-actuator-service

### 3) Inspect deposits and clean gradually

Open the throttle plate by hand (engine OFF) and clean front/rear bore edges and plate perimeter using light cleaner application on cloth/brush.

> High-risk quote: “Clean the throttle actuator bore housing and butterfly both the front and rear sides…” https://www.2carpros.com/articles/throttle-actuator-service

### 4) Avoid over-wetting sensors and seals

Spray minimally, wipe repeatedly, and keep cleaner out of electrical connectors. Do not aggressively pry at the plate or shaft.

### 5) Reassemble intake plumbing

Refit intake ducting and clamps fully. Ensure no vacuum hose is left loose and no cloth/towel remains in the intake path.

### 6) Start and relearn idle behavior

Start engine, allow it to stabilize, then perform a short drive and re-check hot idle.

> Community confirmation quote: “Lots of thick black gunk came out… It idles much more steady now, and my idle droop is GONE!” https://forum.miata.net/vb/showthread.php?t=1344

## Torque Specs / Capacities

- No single universal torque value is provided here because clamp and hardware styles vary by intake setup and market.
- If you removed throttle-body fasteners, torque to your exact NB year/manual specification.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- Stable hot idle with less hunting/droop
- No intake hiss/vacuum leak sound
- No new warning lights after test drive
- Throttle returns cleanly to idle without sticking

## Uncertainty / Open Questions

- Publicly extractable factory manual data in this environment did not yield one definitive NB torque table for all relevant intake/throttle hardware.
- Community experience is consistent on symptom improvement, but improvement magnitude varies with root cause (vacuum leaks, IAC, ignition, fueling).
