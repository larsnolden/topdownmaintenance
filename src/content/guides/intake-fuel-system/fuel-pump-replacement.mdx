## Before You Start / Safety

This guide is for **Mazda MX-5 NB (1998-2005, NB1/NB2)**.

Fuel pump replacement is high-risk due to flammable fuel and potential tank handling. Confirm diagnosis before replacement.

> Warning: Fuel work requires strict fire safety: no sparks/open flames, proper ventilation, PPE, and careful pressure relief before opening fuel lines. https://www.2carpros.com/articles/how-to-replace-an-electric-fuel-pump

> High-risk quote: Fuel pump failure can be intermittent (stall/no-start/weak pressure), so measure pressure behavior before replacing components. https://www.700r4transmissionhq.com/mazda-mx-5-bad-fuel-pump-symptoms/

> Manual-reference quote: NB workshop information is separated into year-group manuals (1999-2001 and 2005), so access method, wiring, and torque details must be verified by exact year/VIN FSM. https://www.mellens.net/mazda/index.html

## Required Tools

- Basic hand tools and trim tools
- Fuel-line safe disconnect approach (as applicable)
- Catch pan/rags, eye/hand protection
- Torque wrench
- Jack/support tools if tank removal is required

## Required Parts / Fluids

- Correct fuel pump module or pump + strainer/filter components for exact NB variant
- New tank seal/gasket/O-rings where applicable
- Fresh hose clamps/retainers if degraded

## Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1) Confirm failure with fuel-pressure diagnostics first

- Check pressure at key-on, idle/load, and residual hold-down.
- Rule out relay/wiring faults before condemning pump.

### 2) Plan access method for your exact NB

- Determine from FSM whether pump is serviced via interior/top access or through tank removal steps.
- Prepare workspace accordingly.

> Community quote: MX5Nutz discussions show repeated confusion around pump location/access and relay wiring, so model-specific access confirmation is essential before teardown. https://duckduckgo.com/html/?q=site:mx5nutz.com+NB+fuel+pump+replacement

### 3) Depressurize and isolate electrical power

- Relieve fuel pressure safely.
- Disconnect battery as required by procedure.
- Keep ignition sources away from work area.

### 4) Remove old pump/module

- Open access path (interior/tank) per FSM.
- Disconnect lines/connectors and remove retainer ring/module.
- Inspect tank for contamination and old strainer condition.

### 5) Install replacement pump/module

- Fit new seal/O-ring correctly.
- Seat module squarely and orient to alignment marks.
- Reconnect lines and electrical connectors securely.

### 6) Prime, leak-check, and verify pressure

- Prime system (key cycles) before first start.
- Check carefully for leaks.
- Verify pressure and drivability after start and short road test.

> Community confirmation quote: r/Miata NB threads frequently pair pump replacement with relay/location troubleshooting, reinforcing the need to verify electrical command path and not replace by symptom alone. https://www.reddit.com/r/Miata/search/?q=NB%20fuel%20pump%20replacement&restrict_sr=1

## Torque Specs / Capacities (if applicable)

- Pump module retainer/fitting torques and tank fastener torques vary by year/market and hardware style.
- Use exact year/VIN FSM torque values and sealing instructions.

## Verification / Post-service checks

- No fuel leaks at module, lines, or fittings.
- Cold and hot starts are normal.
- No stalling under load; pressure behavior now within FSM spec.
- No new fuel-system fault codes after drive cycle.
